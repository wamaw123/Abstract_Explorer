Visual Summary: S245 - Epcoritamab + R-ICE as Salvage Therapy in R/R DLBCL for ASCT Eligibility (EPCORE NHL-2)

Population & Aim (N=31)

R/R DLBCL Pts, ASCT-eligible, ≥1 prior LOT. Aim: Evaluate efficacy & safety of Epcoritamab + R-ICE.

Intervention

Epcoritamab 48mg (step-up C1, QW C1-4, Q2W C5-9, Q4W C10+) + R-ICE (C1-3). Median 4 epcor, 3 R-ICE cycles.

Key Efficacy (N=31)

ORR: 87% (CR 65%, PR 23%). 65% proceeded to ASCT. 6mo: 81% DOR, 74% PFS, 100% OS.

Safety & Conclusion

Manageable. CRS 52% (all G1/2). Neutropenia 74%. No G5 TEAEs. Optimizes salvage, increases ASCT eligibility.

S245 - First disclosure of Epcoritamab + R-ICE in patients with relapsed/refractory diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (R/R DLBCL) eligible for autologous stem cell transplantation (ASCT): EPCORE NHL-2

Raul Cordoba, Marek Trneny, Sven De Vos, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #S245 | Oral Presentation

Background

  • ~50% of LBCL patients (pts) are refractory to salvage chemoimmunotherapy (CIT) like R-ICE.
  • Unmet need to optimize salvage therapy (tx) to increase complete response (CR) rates and ASCT eligibility.
  • CAR-T is an alternative, but challenges (logistics, cost, access, durability) remain.
  • Epcoritamab (epcor), a subcutaneous CD3xCD20 bispecific antibody, showed high CR rates with R-DHAX/C.
  • This is the first report of epcor + R-ICE in R/R DLBCL pts eligible for ASCT (EPCORE NHL-2 trial, NCT04663347).

Methods & Study Design

  • Trial: EPCORE NHL-2 (Phase 1/2).
  • Patients: R/R DLBCL, eligible for R-ICE and ASCT, ≥1 prior line of tx (LOT).
  • Treatment Regimen:
    • Epcoritamab 48 mg SC: Step-up doses C1 (0.16mg, 0.8mg), then full dose QW C1-4; Q2W C5-9; Q4W C10+.
    • R-ICE: Standard doses for Cycles 1-3.
    • Cycle Length: C1-4 (21 days), C5+ (28 days).
  • Treatment Duration: Until ASCT, progressive disease (PD), or unacceptable toxicity.
  • Primary Endpoint: Overall Response Rate (ORR) per investigator (Lugano criteria).
  • Data Cutoff: Dec 18, 2024. Median follow-up: 11 months (range, 6-15).

Patient Characteristics (N=31)

  • Median Age: 62 years (range, 39-77).
  • Male: 55%.
  • Ann Arbor Stage III/IV: 61%.
  • Bulky Disease (≥7 cm): 42%.
  • Prior Lines of Therapy (LOT): 81% had 1 prior LOT (range, 1-3).
  • Progressed within 12 months of 1L tx: 65% (high-risk).

Efficacy Results (N=31, Epcor 48mg + R-ICE)

Median initiated tx cycles: Epcor 4 (range, 2-14); R-ICE 3 (range, 2-3).

  • Overall Response Rate (ORR): 87% (27/31)
  • Complete Response (CR): 65% (20/31)
  • Partial Response (PR): 23% (7/31)
  • High ORR and CR rates observed across subgroups of interest (see Table).

Autologous Stem Cell Transplant (ASCT):

  • Proceeded to ASCT: 65% (20/31 pts).
    • Pre-ASCT status: 13 CR, 7 PR.
    • Post-ASCT: 5 of 7 PRs converted to CR.
  • Did not proceed to ASCT (n=11):
    • 3 (10%) remained on epcor (1 CR, 2 PR).
    • 8 (26%) discontinued (6 PD, 2 other). 6 of these received subsequent tx (3 CAR-T).

Survival Estimates (at 6 months):

  • Duration of Response (DOR): 81% of responses ongoing.
  • Progression-Free Survival (PFS): 74% of pts progression-free.
  • Overall Survival (OS): 100% of pts alive.

Safety Summary (N=31)

  • Most Common TEAEs (any grade):
    • Neutropenia: 74%
    • Anemia: 68%
    • Thrombocytopenia: 68%
  • Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS): 52% (all Grade 1/2, all resolved).
  • Immune Effector Cell-Associated Neurotoxicity Syndrome (ICANS): 1 pt (Grade 1, resolved).
  • Clinical Tumor Lysis Syndrome: None observed.
  • Infections: 58% (18 pts); Serious infections: 16% (5 pts).
  • No discontinuations of epcoritamab due to TEAEs.
  • No Grade 5 TEAEs reported.

Summary & Conclusion

  • Epcoritamab + R-ICE demonstrated high CR rates (65%) and ORR (87%) in ASCT-eligible R/R DLBCL patients, including a high-risk population (65% progressed <12 mo of 1L tx).
  • The safety profile was manageable, with low-grade CRS and no discontinuations due to AEs.
  • A high proportion of patients (65%) were able to proceed to ASCT.
  • These results suggest that epcoritamab combined with CIT may optimize salvage therapy, potentially increasing ASCT eligibility and offering a greater chance of cure compared to historical rates.
