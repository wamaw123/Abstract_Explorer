PB3356 - CD20-negative relapsed/refractory mature B-cell non-Hodgkin lymphomas (B-NHLs) and mature B-cell leukemia in children, adolescents, and young adults: A retrospective review

Luciana Vinti, Jolanda Pianese, Daniela Onofrillo, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PB3356 | Publication Only

Background

  • Common pediatric mature B-cell malignancies: Burkitt lymphoma, DLBCL, PMBCL, mature B-ALL (mB-ALL).
  • Rituximab + chemotherapy improved EFS/OS, but prognosis for R/R disease is dismal.
  • Standard reinduction for R/R pediatric B-NHL/mB-ALL is not established.
  • Novel agents (monoclonal/bispecific CD20-CD3 Abs) are in development.

Aims

To evaluate CD20 expression by immunohistochemistry (IHC) and/or flow cytometry in R/R B-NHLs/mB-ALL in children and young adults, and its potential role in resistance, through an updated retrospective study.

Methods & Study Design

  • Study Type: Retrospective review.
  • Patient Population: Children and young adults with R/R B-NHLs/mB-ALL from 12 Italian Centers over the past 10 years.
  • Data Collection: Baseline data, CD20 status at relapse (biopsy/cytological evaluation), treatment, and outcomes.
  • CD20 Expression Analysis: IHC and/or flow cytometry on samples at relapse.

Patient Characteristics & CD20 Status (N=27)

  • Median Age at Diagnosis: 11.4 years (range, 4.1-19.3).
  • Median Age at Relapse: 12.3 years (range, 4.6-21.3).
  • Diagnoses: Burkitt Lymphoma (most common), DLBCL, mB-ALL, PMBCL.
  • Prior Rituximab: 24/27 patients (median 6 infusions).
  • Time to Relapse: <100 days for 20/27 patients (median 1.8 months from last treatment).
  • CD20 Loss at Relapse: 40% (11/27 patients) by IHC and/or flow cytometry (all were CD20 positive at diagnosis). Biopsy mostly at first relapse (15 pts).

Outcomes Based on CD20 Status at Relapse

CD20-Negative Patients (n=10*):

  • Died of progressive disease: 7.
  • Partial Response (on treatment): 2 (1 Burkitt, 1 DLBCL).
  • Alive and disease-free: 1 (experienced Hodgkin lymphoma as second cancer).
  • (*Abstract mentions 11/27 CD20 loss, then details outcomes for 10 CD20-negative patients. This discrepancy is noted from the source.)

CD20-Positive Patients (n=17):

  • Died: 7.
  • Alive: 10 (6 CR, 4 PR).

Hematopoietic Stem Cell Transplantation (HSCT):

  • 7 patients received HSCT (5 allogeneic, 2 autologous).
  • 2 of these had CD20-negative relapse.
  • 3 patients post-alloHSCT are alive and disease-free.

Summary & Conclusion

  • Loss of CD20 expression at relapse occurred in 40% of pediatric/young adult patients with R/R B-NHLs/mB-ALL in this retrospective cohort.
  • CD20-loss was characterized by a more aggressive disease course and worse outcomes compared to patients who remained CD20-positive at relapse.
  • Given the potential benefit of CD20-targeted therapies (monoclonal or bispecific antibodies), these findings underscore the importance of histological re-evaluation, including CD20 status assessment, whenever possible at relapse to guide appropriate targeted therapy selection.
