PB3304 - Efficacy and safety of sequential R-CDOP and R-CMOP regimens in DLBCL patients with IPI score 0-1 and bulky mass: A retrospective analysis

Liufang Gu, Jin Hou, Jing Yan, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PB3304 | Publication Only

Background

  • DLBCL is the most prevalent aggressive B-cell lymphoma.
  • R-CDOP achieves ~60% cure rate, but low-risk (IPI 0-1) DLBCL with bulky masses show suboptimal outcomes.
  • Standard R-CDOP often results in poor PFS/OS for patients with ≤PR at mid-treatment.

Aims

To retrospectively evaluate the efficacy and safety of sequential R-CMOP reinduction therapy in low-risk DLBCL patients (IPI/aaIPI 0-1) with bulky masses who achieved ≤PR after four cycles of R-CDOP.

Methods & Study Design

  • Retrospective analysis of 17 DLBCL patients (IPI/aaIPI 0-1) with bulky masses.
  • Initial Treatment: 4 cycles of R-CDOP (Rituximab 375 mg/m² D0; Cyclophosphamide 750 mg/m² D1, Cisplatin 25 mg/m² D1, Vincristine 1.3 mg/m² D1; Prednisone 100 mg D1-5).
  • Reinduction for ≤PR: Patients with PR or SD on MRI/CT or PET-CT after R-CDOP were switched to 4 cycles of R-CMOP (Liposomal Mitoxantrone 18 mg/m² D1; other agents/doses identical to R-CDOP, i.e., Rituximab, Cyclophosphamide, Vincristine, Prednisone).

Efficacy Results (Post R-CMOP, N=17)

  • After 2 cycles of R-CMOP: 9/17 patients (52.9%) achieved CR.
  • After 4 cycles of R-CMOP (Best Response):
    • CR: 12/17 patients (70.6%)
    • SD: 3/17 patients (17.6%)
    • PD: 2/17 patients (11.8%)

Safety Summary (Post R-CMOP)

  • Hematologic toxicity (primarily Grade 1-2 leukopenia) was common.
  • Neutropenia: 2 patients (resolved with G-CSF). Grade not specified.
  • Infusion-related reactions to liposomal mitoxantrone: 1 patient (managed symptomatically).
  • No hepatic or renal toxicity observed.
  • Five patients with preexisting cardiovascular comorbidities tolerated liposomal mitoxantrone without exacerbation.
  • Safety profiles were comparable between R-CDOP and R-CMOP.
  • Reduced cardiotoxicity and myelosuppression observed (compared to standard anthracyclines, implied).
  • No new safety signals.

Summary & Conclusion

  • For low-risk DLBCL (IPI/aaIPI 0-1) with bulky masses and suboptimal mid-treatment response (≤PR) to R-CDOP, sequential R-CMOP reinduction demonstrates promising efficacy and safety.
  • Liposomal mitoxantrone offers advantages: non-cross resistance, tumor-targeted accumulation, prolonged half-life, enhanced antitumor activity, reduced cardiotoxicity/myelosuppression.
  • Long-term outcomes require validation through larger randomized controlled trials.
