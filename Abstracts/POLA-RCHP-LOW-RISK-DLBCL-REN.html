Visual Summary: PS1967 - Pola-R-CHP in 1L Low-Risk DLBCL (IPI 0-1)

Population & Aim (N=72)

Previously untreated low-risk DLBCL (IPI 0-1). Aim: Assess Pola-R-CHP effectiveness & safety.

Intervention

Pola-R-CHP regimen (POLARIX doses/schedule). Median 5.5 cycles.

Key Efficacy

EOT CRR (N=32): 90.6%
Interim CRR (N=61): 78.7%
ORR (Interim & EOT): 100%

Safety & Conclusion

Manageable safety. Most AEs G1-2. G3-4 Neutropenia (20.8%). Very high response rate in low-risk DLBCL.

PS1967 - Effectiveness and safety of Pola-R-CHP regimen in previously untreated low-risk DLBCL: A nationwide multicenter retrospective study of IPI 0-1 patients in China

Yuhong Ren, Jia Liu, Zhijuan Lin, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PS1967 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Pola-R-CHP improved PFS in 1L DLBCL with IPI ≥2 (POLARIX study), becoming a standard for them.
  • Patients with IPI 0-1 (26-34% of 1L DLBCL) have insufficient evidence for Pola-R-CHP use.
  • This study addresses the knowledge gap for this low-risk group.

Aims

To assess the effectiveness and safety of Pola-R-CHP regimen in newly diagnosed DLBCL patients with an IPI score of 0-1 in a real-world setting, aiming to fill the evidence gap and support clinical practice.

Methods & Study Design

  • Nationwide, multicenter (12 Chinese hospitals) retrospective study.
  • Enrollment: May 2023 - Feb 2025.
  • Patients: Previously untreated DLBCL with IPI 0-1.
  • Intervention: Pola-R-CHP (same doses/schedule as POLARIX).
  • Assessment: PET/CT or CT at Interim (3/4 cycles) & EOT (6 cycles).
  • Response: Lugano 2014 criteria. AEs: NCI-CTCAE 5.0.
  • Primary Endpoint: CR rate at EOT.
  • Secondary Endpoints: Interim CRR, ORR, AEs, PFS, OS.

Efficacy Results

  • Primary Endpoint: CR rate at EOT (N=32 evaluable): 90.6%
  • Interim CR rate (N=61 evaluable): 78.7%
  • ORR at Interim (N=61): 100%
  • ORR at EOT (N=32): 100%
  • Subgroup analyses showed similar response rates at interim and EOT across various subgroups (age, COO, IPI, DE, etc.).
  • Median follow-up: 6.0 months (range 0.2-11.2).
  • Progression: 1 patient at 7.5 months.

Safety Summary (N=72)

Most common Adverse Events (AEs), mostly Grade 1-2:

  • Anemia: 72.2%
  • Decreased WBC count: 47.2%
  • Decreased neutrophil count: 40.3%
  • Elevated liver enzymes: 18.1%

Most common Grade 3-4 AE:

  • Decreased neutrophil count: 20.8%

Summary & Conclusion

  • This is reported as the first multicenter retrospective study of Pola-R-CHP as 1L treatment for DLBCL patients with IPI 0-1 worldwide.
  • Pola-R-CHP achieved a very high response rate and manageable safety profile, even among those with high-risk factors within this low IPI cohort.
  • Conclusions need validation by prospective studies with longer follow-up and larger sample size due to retrospective nature and short follow-up.
