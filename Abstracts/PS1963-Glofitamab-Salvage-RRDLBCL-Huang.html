PS1963 - Glofitamab combined with salvage therapy for relapsed or refractory diffuse large B cell lymphoma: A real-world prospective observational study in Chinese patients

Haiwen Huang, Jin Zhou, Tao You

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PS1963 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Glofitamab, a CD20xCD3 bispecific antibody, has shown remarkable effectiveness in R/R DLBCL in clinical trials (e.g., NCT03075696), leading to high CR rates and durable responses.
  • Real-world data on glofitamab, especially in combination with salvage therapies for high-risk patients, is limited.

Aims

To explore the efficacy and safety of Glofitamab combined with salvage therapy in Chinese patients with R/R DLBCL in a real-world setting through a prospective observational study.

Methods & Study Design

  • Study Type: Real-world prospective observational study (NCT06497452). Approved by IRB (20240430041615581).
  • Patient Population: R/R DLBCL patients aged ≥18 years.
  • Intervention: Glofitamab combined with salvage therapy (e.g., DHAP, GemOx, EPOCH regimen).
    • Obinutuzumab 1000mg IV on Cycle 1 Day 1 (C1D1) as pretreatment.
    • Glofitamab step-up dosing: C1D8 (2.5mg), C1D15 (10mg).
    • Glofitamab full dose: 30mg from C2D1.
    • Salvage therapy given at standard dose per protocol.
  • Assessments: Safety evaluated each cycle. Efficacy by PET-CT at screening and every two cycles.
  • Data Cutoff: December 1, 2024.

Efficacy Results (DLBCL Pts, N=36 Evaluable)

  • Overall Response Rate (ORR): 72.2% (26/36)
  • Complete Response (CR) Rate: 38.8% (14/36)
  • Partial Response (PR) Rate: 33.3% (12/36) (Calculated)
  • Burkitt's lymphoma (n=1 evaluable): PR
  • All CRs were ongoing at data cut-off.
  • Median OS and median PFS were not reached.

Safety Summary (N=42)

  • Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS): 38 (90.5%) patients.
    • Mostly Grade 1 (80.1% of all patients, calculated as 38*0.801 for specific G1 count, or 90.5% * X = G1, if 80.1% refers to proportion of CRS events. Assuming 80.1% of CRS events were G1). Abstract states "Most of the CRS events were low grade (Grade 1: 80.1%)" - this suggests 80.1% of the 90.5% were G1. Actual G1 count: 38 * 0.801 ≈ 30 patients. The remaining were G2.
  • Immune Effector Cell-Associated Neurotoxicity Syndrome (ICANS): 3 patients.
  • Immune-related Myocardial Injury: 1 patient.
  • Most Common Grade 3/4 AE: Neutropenia (23/42; 54.8%), mainly due to combined chemotherapy.
  • No Grade 5 AEs reported.
  • No discontinuations of glofitamab-based therapy due to AEs.

Summary & Conclusion

  • Preliminary results from this prospective real-world study demonstrate the effectiveness and safety of glofitamab-based salvage therapy for heavily pre-treated R/R DLBCL patients with high-risk prognostic factors.
  • Updated analyses, including further follow-up data and exploratory applications of glofitamab, will be presented.
