Visual Summary: PB3233 - R-TP (Rituximab, Thiotepa, Pomalidomide) in R/R PCNS-DLBCL

Population & Aim (N=16)

R/R PCNS-DLBCL Pts post 1L chemo. Aim: Evaluate response rate & tolerance of R-TP regimen.

Intervention (R-TP Regimen)

Rituximab (375mg/m²), Thiotepa (60mg/m²) D1; Pomalidomide 4mg PO D1-14. Q21D for up to 6 cycles.

Key Efficacy (mF/U 8mo)

Post 2 cycles (N=16): ORR 100% (CR 0%). Post 6 cycles (N=11): ORR 100%, CRR 72.7%.

Safety & Conclusion

Generally mild hematologic toxicity. R-TP shows high efficacy & good tolerability; promising for R/R PCNS-DLBCL.

PB3233 - High-dose Thiotepa combined with Rituximab and Pomalidomide is a perfect alternative chemotherapy regimen for relapsed/refractory primary central nervous system diffuse large B cell lymphoma

Xia Zhao, Zhihe Liu

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PB3233 | Publication Only

Background

  • Primary Central Nervous System Diffuse Large B-cell Lymphoma (PCNS-DLBCL) is rare, aggressive, with poor prognosis.
  • First-line treatment: Multi-drug chemotherapy + high-dose methotrexate (MTX).
  • Over 50% of patients experience relapse or refractory (R/R) disease.
  • No established standard of care for R/R PCNS-DLBCL after first-line chemotherapy.

Aims

To evaluate the treatment response rate and tolerance of the R-TP regimen (Rituximab, Thiotepa, Pomalidomide) in patients with R/R PCNS-DLBCL after first-line chemotherapy.

Methods & Study Design

  • Study Type: Single-center study (China).
  • Patient Population: R/R PCNS-DLBCL patients treated between Jan 2023 - Dec 2024.
  • R-TP Regimen (21-day cycles, up to 6 cycles for Pomalidomide):
    • Rituximab: 375 mg/m² (Day 1 of each cycle).
    • Thiotepa: 60 mg/m² (Day 1 of each cycle).
    • Pomalidomide: 4 mg PO daily (Days 1-14 of each of the first six cycles).
  • Data Collected: Baseline data, overall response rates, adverse events.
  • Data Cutoff: February 2025.

Patient Characteristics (N=16)

  • Median Age: 66 years (range, 49-74).
  • Gender Ratio (Male:Female): 5:3.
  • IELSG Risk Score:
    • Low-risk: 18.8%
    • Intermediate-risk: 81.2%
  • Baseline Hematology: WBC, Hemoglobin, Platelets mostly normal at enrollment.
  • Hans Algorithm Subtype: Non-GCB 87.5%.

Efficacy Results (Median Follow-up: 8 months)

All patients received at least 2 cycles of R-TP.

  • After 2 Cycles (N=16):
    • Overall Response Rate (ORR): 100%.
    • Complete Response (CR) Rate: 0%.
  • After 6 Cycles (N=11 completed):
    • Overall Response Rate (ORR): 100%.
    • Complete Response (CR) Rate: 72.7%.

Safety Summary

  • Common adverse events were generally mild hematologic toxicity.

Summary & Conclusion

  • The R-TP regimen (Rituximab, Thiotepa, Pomalidomide) demonstrated high efficacy in patients with R/R PCNS-DLBCL, with a 100% ORR after 2 cycles and a 72.7% CRR in patients completing 6 cycles.
  • The treatment showed good tolerability, with common adverse events being generally mild hematologic toxicity.
  • R-TP offers a promising treatment option for this challenging patient population.
