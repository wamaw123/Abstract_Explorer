PS2144 - AUC fludarabine and product choice are main predictors of CRS/ICANS after CAR T cell therapy: Generation of an improved model

Keven Hoerster, Jan Pelster, Marcel Teichert, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PS2144 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS) and Immune Effector Cell-Associated Neurotoxicity Syndrome (ICANS) are known side effects of CAR T-cell therapy.
  • Elevated modified Endothelial Activation and Stress Index score (mEASIX) is associated with increased odds of CRS/ICANS.
  • Higher effective dose of fludarabine during lymphodepletion (AUC fludarabine) predicts CAR-T outcomes and is linked to ICANS.
  • The combined effect of mEASIX and AUC fludarabine in a prediction model, especially across various CAR-T products (including liso-cel) and in Multiple Myeloma (MM) patients, has not been fully investigated.

Aims

To establish multivariable prediction models for CRS (≥°II) and ICANS (≥°II) based on mEASIX, AUC fludarabine, and specific CAR-T product, encompassing all currently registered products.

Methods & Study Design

  • Patient Cohort: Data from 176 CAR-T patients.
  • Initial Predictors Considered: AUC fludarabine, mEASIX, gender, age, weight, liso-cel, brexu-cel, and MM diagnosis.
  • AUC Fludarabine Estimation: Using eGFR (CKD-EPI-2021), patient weight, and total fludarabine dose via Langenhorst et al. (2018) model.
  • mEASIX Calculation: log2(mEASIX) scores as previously described (Pennisi et al., 2021).
  • Data Handling: Continuous variables centered and scaled.
  • Modeling: Python v3.12 (numpy/pandas, sklearn for ROC, statsmodel's logit function). Two multivariable logistic regression models fitted using maximum likelihood estimation for ICANS ≥°II and CRS ≥°II.

Results: Predictive Models for CRS & ICANS

Two multivariable logistic regression models were developed based on data from 176 CAR-T patients. Gender, patient weight, and brexu-cel were excluded as predictors in the final models.

ICANS ≥°II Model Predictors:

  • AUC Fludarabine: Significantly associated with ICANS (OR: 2.3, 95%CI: 1.32-4.00, p=0.003).
  • Liso-cel: Reduced odds of ICANS (OR: 0.10, 95%CI: 0.02-0.43, p=0.002).
  • Multiple Myeloma (MM): Reduced odds of ICANS (OR: 0.02, 95%CI: 0.002-0.18, p<0.001).
  • Age: Trend towards higher odds in elderly (OR: 1.81, 95%CI: 0.97-3.36, p=0.059).
  • mEASIX: Trend towards higher odds with increased mEASIX (OR: 1.46, 95%CI: 0.94-2.27, p=0.089).

CRS ≥°II Model Predictors (Age excluded):

  • AUC Fludarabine: Significantly higher odds of CRS (OR: 1.72, 95%CI: 1.14-2.58, p=0.008).
  • Liso-cel: Lower risk of CRS (OR: 0.15, 95%CI: 0.06-0.39, p<0.001).
  • Multiple Myeloma (MM): Lower risk of CRS (OR: 0.25, 95%CI: 0.11-0.59, p=0.001).
  • mEASIX: No significantly higher risk (OR: 1.28, 95%CI: 0.90-1.81, p=0.16).

Model Performance:

  • ICANS Model ROC AUC: 0.86
  • CRS Model ROC AUC: 0.75

Summary & Conclusion

  • Elevated AUC fludarabine was significantly associated with an increased risk of severe CRS and ICANS.
  • Treatment with lisocabtagene maraleucel (liso-cel) and a diagnosis of Multiple Myeloma (MM) were associated with a decreased risk of severe CRS/ICANS.
  • The previously reported association of mEASIX with CRS/ICANS was less pronounced in these multivariable models.
  • ROC curves derived from the model predictions demonstrated good predictive performance for both ICANS (AUC 0.86) and CRS (AUC 0.75).
  • The authors plan to confirm these results in an external validation cohort with additional predictors.
