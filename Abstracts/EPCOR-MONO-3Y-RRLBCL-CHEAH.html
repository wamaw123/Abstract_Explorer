PF920 - Sustained remission in relapsed or refractory diffuse large B-cell lymphoma with Epcoritamab monotherapy: EPCORE NHL-1 3-Y results and novel subgroup analyses in patients with complete response at 2 Y

Chan Y. Cheah, Yasmin H. Karimi, Julie M. Vose, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PF920 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Depth and duration of complete response (CR) correlate with long-term outcomes in large B-cell lymphoma (LBCL).
  • Epcoritamab, a subcutaneous CD3xCD20 bispecific antibody, showed deep, durable responses and manageable safety in pts with R/R LBCL in the pivotal phase 2 EPCORE® NHL-1 trial (NCT03625037).
  • This report presents 3-year follow-up results and a post hoc analysis of pts remaining in CR at 2 years after starting treatment.

Methods & Study Design

  • Trial: EPCORE NHL-1 (Phase 2, NCT03625037).
  • Patients (N=157): R/R CD20+ LBCL, ≥2 prior lines of therapy (LOT).
  • Treatment Regimen: Epcoritamab monotherapy SC in 28-day cycles (C).
    • C1: Step-up doses (0.16mg, 0.8mg), then 48mg full dose.
    • C1-3: QW dosing.
    • C4-9: Q2W dosing.
    • C≥10: Q4W dosing.
  • Treatment Duration: Until progressive disease (PD) or unacceptable toxicity.
  • Primary Endpoint: Overall Response Rate (ORR) by Lugano criteria (investigator assessed for long-term outcomes).
  • Data Cutoff: May 3, 2024 (median follow-up: 37.1 months).

Patient Characteristics (Pts in CR at 2 Years, n=32)

Of 65 pts who achieved CR, 49% (32/65) remained in CR at 2 years.

  • Median Age: 63 years.
  • Male: 47%.
  • Ann Arbor Stage III/IV: 81%.
  • Prior CAR-T Therapy: 38%.
  • Refractory to ≥2 Prior LOT: 66%.

Efficacy Results (Median Follow-up: 37.1 months)

Overall Population (N=157):

  • Overall Response Rate (ORR): 59% (92/157).
  • Complete Response (CR): 41% (65/157).
  • Median Duration of Response (mDOR): 20.8 months (95% CI, 13.0-32.0).
  • Median Duration of CR (mDoCR): 36.1 months (95% CI, 20.1-Not Reached [NR]).
  • Median Progression-Free Survival (mPFS): 4.2 months (95% CI, 2.8-5.5).
  • Median Overall Survival (mOS): 18.5 months (95% CI, 11.7-27.7).

All Patients with CR (n=65):

  • mPFS: 37.3 months (95% CI, 26.0-NR).
  • mOS: NR (95% CI, 36.4-NR).

Patients in CR at 2 Years (n=32):

  • At 36 months: 96% were progression-free and 97% were alive.

Treatment Discontinuation & Management:

  • 138 pts (88%) discontinued tx (58% PD, 4% transplant).
  • 32 pts with CR discontinued epcoritamab for reasons other than PD/death (median tx duration 13.2 mo; median time in CR post-discontinuation 9.6 mo for 17 pts with scans).
  • 19 pts (12%) remained on epcoritamab at data cutoff.
  • 15 CR pts had temporary tx pause >6 wks (median 70.5 d); all maintained CR upon reinitiation.

Safety Summary (N=157)

  • Safety profile consistent with previous reports.
  • Most Common TEAEs (any grade):
    • Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS): 51%
    • Fatigue: 25%
    • Pyrexia: 25%
  • Incidence and severity of CRS and ICANS were unchanged from previous reports.
  • Serious infections: 31%.

Summary & Conclusion

  • At 3-year follow-up, epcoritamab monotherapy demonstrated long-term disease remission and the potential for cure in some patients with 3L+ R/R LBCL.
  • Patients who achieved a CR, particularly those remaining in CR at 2 years, had excellent long-term PFS and OS.
  • The long-term safety profile was consistent with the established safety profile of epcoritamab, with manageable TEAEs.
  • Temporary treatment interruptions for over 6 weeks did not compromise CR in responding patients.
  • Additional data will be presented to further characterize pts with R/R LBCL who achieved long-term remission.
