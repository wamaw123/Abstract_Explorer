Loncastuximab in Relapsed/Refractory Marginal Zone Lymphoma

Updated Analysis of a Phase 2 Multicenter Study (NCT05296070)

Izidore S. Lossos, et al. | 17th ICML 2023 | Poster #260

Background & Aim

  • Marginal zone lymphoma (MZL) is a heterogeneous disease where achieving complete response (CR) is a key prognostic factor[cite: 13].
  • This is an ongoing, open-label, multi-institutional, investigator-initiated study evaluating Lonca in R/R MZL.
  • The primary endpoint is CR rate after 6 cycles of treatment.

Methods

  • Adults with R/R MZL requiring treatment after â‰¥1 line of systemic therapy were eligible.
  • The study uses a Bayesian optimal phase 2 design with interim analyses.
  • Dosing: Lonca was administered IV Q3W for 6 cycles (0.15 mg/kg for 2 cycles, then 0.075 mg/kg for 4 cycles).
  • Required premedication included dexamethasone and spironolactone for fluid overload prophylaxis.
  • Response was assessed by PET-CT or MRI/CT based on Lugano 2014 criteria[cite: 15, 17].

Efficacy in High-Risk Subgroups

  • CR was achieved in 8 of 13 (62%) patients with POD24.
  • One patient who had progressed after prior CAR-T therapy achieved a CR.

Dose reductions occurred in 3 patients (11%). One patient (4%) discontinued due to cholestatic hepatitis and subsequently fully recovered.

Conclusions

  • Lonca demonstrates clinically meaningful activity with a robust CR rate in patients with R/R MZL.
  • The treatment is well tolerated, with a safety profile consistent with known adverse effects and no new unexpected toxicities.
  • Patients demonstrated durable complete responses, with an 18-month ongoing CR rate of 83%[cite: 26, 33].
  • The study passed its first and second interim futility analysis thresholds.
