PF1018 - Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL): Prognostic impact of CD30 expression and its association with the tumor microenvironment

Ken Naganuma, Yuka Tanaka, Noriyuki Sakata, et al. (KN and YT contributed equally and share the first authorship)

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PF1018 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) is the most common type of lymphoma, but it is highly heterogeneous, and the prognosis varies among cases.
  • Although R-CHOP therapy is the standard first-line treatment, more specific therapies are necessary for the individual.
  • Brentuximab vedotin (BV) is an anti-CD30 antibody-drug conjugate that has been reported to be effective with fewer side effects in other lymphomas.
  • Although the efficacy of BV is reportedly not correlated with CD30 expression in tumor cells, and it is effective even in CD30-negative cases, CD30 evaluation has been limited to pathological assessment by immunohistochemistry (IHC).
  • To date, there have been no reports of quantitative evaluation of CD30 gene expression in tumor cells using microarray analysis or protein levels by flow cytometry.

Aims

To identify optimal candidates for BV therapy in DLBCL, we investigated the prognostic significance, clinical features, and molecular basis of CD30-high DLBCL based on gene and protein expression analysis.

Methods & Study Design

This study involved analysis of publicly available and institutional data to assess CD30 expression in DLBCL.

Key Findings: CD30 Gene Expression (GSE117556, N=928)

  • CD30-High Definition: 59% of DLBCL cases (n=545) were CD30-high by microarray and ROC analysis (event: death).
    • Median CD30 mRNA: CD30-high 9.45 vs. CD30-low 7.35.
  • Prognostic Impact:
    • CD30-high DLBCL was an independent favorable prognostic factor (HR 0.77 [0.60-0.98], p=0.03), irrespective of sex and IPI.
    • CD30-high GCB subtype showed a particularly favorable prognosis (HR 0.48 [0.27-0.85], p=0.01).
  • Molecular Subtypes & CD30 Expression:
    • HGBCL: Lowest CD30 mRNA expression.
    • UNC type: Significantly highest CD30 mRNA expression.
  • Tumor Microenvironment (TME - CIBERSORT):
    • HGBCL had the least T cell infiltration, whereas UNC type had the most.
    • CD30 mRNA expression correlated positively with CD4+ T cells and negatively with CD8+ T cells.
    • These results suggest that CD30-high DLBCL is frequently of the UNC type and has an immune hot TME with a predominance of CD4+ T cells.
  • The high prevalence of CD30-high DLBCL (59%) aligns with BV's efficacy in IHC CD30-negative cases in trials.

Key Findings: CD30 Protein Expression (Institutional Cohort, N=171 by FCM)

  • CD30-High Definition: 28% of cases showed high CD30 protein expression by flow cytometry.
  • Prognostic Impact:
    • High CD30 protein expression was a favorable prognostic factor (HR 0.67 [0.38-1.19], p=0.17).
    • In the GCB type, high CD30 protein expression showed a trend towards favorable prognosis (HR 0.32 [0.10-1.06], p=0.06).
  • These protein-level findings are consistent with the gene expression results.

Summary & Conclusion

  • These findings suggest that CD30 evaluation by FCM can identify favorable prognostic cases, excluding HGBCL, within the GCB type.
  • Furthermore, our study suggests the possibility of improving treatment outcomes in the UNC type of CD30-high DLBCL by adding bispecific antibodies, lenalidomide or immune checkpoint inhibitors to BV.
