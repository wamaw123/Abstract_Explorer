BISPECIFIC ANTIBODIES AS HOLDING/BRIDGING THERAPY BEFORE CAR-T IN LBCL

Investigating Safety and Efficacy in a Retrospective Multicenter Study

Hanna Katharina Zieger, et al. | EHA Library PF944 | Abstract Release: 05/14/25 | Presentation: 06/13/2025

Background

  • Bispecific antibodies (bABs) are increasingly used as bridging or holding therapy prior to CAR-T cell therapy in Large B-Cell Lymphoma (LBCL).
  • This is particularly relevant for patients refractory to initial holding/bridging regimens.
  • The impact of this strategy on CAR-T safety and efficacy is unclear, with concerns about potential T-cell exhaustion versus synergistic effects.

Study Aims

To retrospectively investigate the safety and efficacy of bABs used as bridging or holding therapy prior to CAR-T in patients with LBCL.

Methods & Study Design

  • Retrospective study of patients from 6 German centers.
  • Data collected: Patient characteristics, prior treatments, PFS, OS, safety profiles of bABs and CAR-T (CRS, ICANS, infections, ICU use, tocilizumab use).
  • Response assessment: PET-CT by local investigators (Lugano criteria).

Summary & Conclusion

  • With limitations of small sample size and short follow-up, these data suggest that bridging therapy with bABs before CAR-T therapy is safe and effective in LBCL.
  • More detailed data in a larger group of patients with sufficient follow-up time will be presented at the conference.
