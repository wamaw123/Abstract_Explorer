PF980 - Efficacy of Pola-R-CHP in previously untreated CD5-positive diffuse large B-cell lymphoma

Junqing Xu, Jingrui Sui, Lijie Yan, Peiqi Zhao, Xia Zhao, Xiaofeng Luo, xiangyan Feng, Yuanfeng Zhang, Kaimin Li

EHA2025 Congress | Abstract #PF980 | Poster Presentation | Session: Aggressive Non-Hodgkin lymphoma - Clinical

Background

  • CD5-positive diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) is a high-risk subtype with a poor prognosis.
  • In April 2023, Pola-R-CHP (polatuzumab vedotin, rituximab, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, and prednisone) was approved in China for the first-line (1L) treatment of DLBCL.

Aims

This study aimed to evaluate the efficacy of Pola-R-CHP as a first-line therapy for CD5-positive DLBCL.

Methods & Study Design

  • Retrospective enrollment of previously untreated CD5-positive DLBCL patients from April 2023 to May 2024 across four institutions.
  • A matched comparison was conducted between Pola-R-CHP and R-CHOP using a DLBCL database from the investigators' institute, covering January 2017 to January 2024.
  • Treatment efficacy assessed via PET/CT scans after 3 cycles (interim) and 6 cycles of R-chemo plus 2 cycles of R (end of treatment - EOT).
  • Statistical tests: Chi-square test and Log-rank test for response rates and progression-free survival (PFS).

Efficacy Results

All patients completed treatment and underwent PET-CT assessments. Median follow-up for Pola-R-CHP group was 11.7 months.

Survival Outcomes

  • Median PFS and OS in the Pola-R-CHP group were not reached.
  • 6-month PFS: Pola-R-CHP 96.3% vs R-CHOP 87.7% (NS).
  • 12-month PFS: Pola-R-CHP 85.2% vs R-CHOP 78.1% (NS).

Safety Summary (Pola-R-CHP Group, N=27)

  • Grade 3 and 4 adverse events (AEs) reported in 9 of 27 (33.3%) patients.
  • Most common Grade 3/4 AE: Neutropenia.
  • Other side effects: Peripheral neuropathy and thrombocytopenia.
  • Two patients experienced fatal disease progression during the study despite subsequent CAR-T cell therapy and CD20xCD3 bispecific antibody treatment.

Summary & Conclusion

  • CD5-positive DLBCL patients exhibited rapid responses to Pola-R-CHP.
  • The Pola-R-CHP treatment demonstrated an acceptable safety profile in this high-risk population.
  • Long-term progression-free survival benefits are anticipated with Pola-R-CHP in CD5-positive DLBCL, although extended follow-up is necessary to confirm these findings.
