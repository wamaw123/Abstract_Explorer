PF1311 - LymphoPath: AI-driven digital pathology platform for risk stratification in diffuse large B-CELL lymphoma

Adrián Mosquera Orgueira, Manuel Piñeiro Fiel, Rosana Abal García, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PF1311 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) is a heterogeneous malignancy with variable treatment responses, complicating risk stratification.
  • Traditional histopathology is qualitative and often lacks reproducibility.
  • AI-driven image analysis enables quantitative biomarker extraction, refining prognosis and personalizing treatment.
  • LymphoPath is an AI-powered platform integrating digital pathology and image feature extraction to enhance risk prediction and therapy optimization in DLBCL.

Aims

  • To automatically identify and quantify tumor cells and microenvironmental components in DLBCL histopathology.
  • To extract morphological and geospatial features that capture their interactions.
  • To convert these features into reproducible AI-based biomarkers.
  • To correlate these biomarkers with clinical outcomes in patients treated with R-CHOP and similar regimens.

Methods & Study Design

  • Study Type: AI-based pipeline development and validation using retrospective patient data.
  • Patient Population: 118 DLBCL patients (2000-2021).
  • Image Analysis:
    • ML-based polygonal annotations by expert hematopathologists.
    • Over 20,000 cells classified into tumor and microenvironmental populations.
    • A pixel classifier distinguished lymphoid from non-lymphoid tissue.
    • The trained model was applied to digitized whole-slide images (60X magnification).
  • Feature Extraction: Quantitative features including nuclear morphology, chromatin structure, staining patterns, and spatial relationships (e.g., Delaunay clusters).
  • Statistical Analysis: Random Survival Forest (RSF) used to predict survival, focusing on medians for robustness.
  • Performance Assessment: Out-of-bag (OOB) concordance-index metrics.

LymphoPath AI Pipeline Workflow

Input Data Histopathological slides from 118 DLBCL Pts
▼
Expert Annotation & Cell Classification >20,000 cells (tumor/microenvironment) Pixel classification (lymphoid/non-lymphoid)
▼

▼
Survival Prediction Modeling Random Survival Forest (RSF) - Model 1: Tumor cell features - Model 2: Microenvironment features - Model 3: Combined features
Performance: OOB Concordance-Index Correlation with Clinical Outcomes

Results: Predictive Model Performance

Predictive performance was evaluated using Out-of-Bag (OOB) scores for three variable sets:

  • Model 1 (Tumor cell morphology & spatial properties): OOB score 0.595.
  • Model 2 (Microenvironment cell morphology): OOB score 0.552 (less predictive power alone).
  • Model 3 (Combined Tumor + Microenvironment): OOB score 0.684 (highest predictive capacity).

These results highlight the importance of integrating tumor-intrinsic and microenvironmental features for more accurate patient risk stratification.

Key Predictive Variables Identified:

  • Cellular positioning
  • Spatial relationships between clusters
  • Cluster size
  • Staining variability

These features capture critical morphological and structural patterns, reinforcing the value of multimodal data integration.

Summary & Conclusion

  • LymphoPath leverages AI-driven histopathology to refine risk stratification and personalize treatment in DLBCL.
  • Integrating tumor and microenvironmental features significantly enhances survival prediction capabilities.
  • The spatial relationships between cellular components proved to be highly influential in the predictive models.
  • The combined model (tumor + microenvironment) demonstrated the strongest predictive power, emphasizing the need for a holistic approach to risk assessment in DLBCL.
  • Ongoing validation efforts support LymphoPath as a potentially key tool for optimizing therapeutic strategies and improving the management of DLBCL patients.
