PS2007 - Genomic testing in patients with large cell and follicular lymphoma treated with T-cell engagers bi-specific antibodies and correlation with treatment response

Rita Tavarozzi, Alfredo Rivas Delgado, Michelle Okwali, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PS2007 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Bispecific antibodies (bsAbs) have transformed treatment for relapsed/refractory (R/R) B-cell non-Hodgkin lymphoma (B-NHL).
  • bsAbs engage T-cells to target malignant B-cells, showing efficacy even in heavily pretreated patients (pts).
  • Real-world evidence for bsAbs is limited, and the role of genomic testing in optimizing outcomes is not fully defined.

Aims

To evaluate the real-world efficacy of bsAbs in R/R B-NHL and explore the role of genomic testing in optimizing patient outcomes.

Methods & Study Design

  • Study Type: Retrospective, single-center (Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center).
  • Patient Population (N=83 total, 82 analyzed): R/R B-NHL pts treated with commercially available bsAbs (06/2022 - 11/2024).
    • R/R Diffuse Large B-Cell Lymphoma (DLBCL): 58 pts.
    • R/R Follicular Lymphoma (FL): 24 pts.
    • All treated after at least two prior therapy lines (PTL).
  • Genomic Testing: Last available pre-bsAb tumor biopsy underwent NGS (MSK-IMPACT-Heme).
  • Data Collected: Clinical characteristics, treatment history, genomic profiles, survival outcomes.
  • Statistical Analysis: Fisher’s exact test for group comparisons.

Patient Characteristics (N=82)

  • Male: 56%.
  • Stage IV Disease: 82%.
  • Age > 60 years: 77%.
  • R/R after immunotherapy: 100%.
  • Median Prior Therapy Lines (PTL): 4.
  • Prior ASCT: 12%.
  • R/R after CAR-T: 39%.

Efficacy & Genomic Results

Response to bsAbs:

  • R/R DLBCL (n=58):
    • Epcoritamab (Epco, n=29): ORR 59%, CR 28%.
    • Glofitamab (Glofit, n=25): ORR 50%, CR 30%.
    • Mosunetuzumab (Mosun, n=4): ORR/CR not individually specified but overall DLBCL ORR 56%, CR 31%.
    • DLBCL R/R after CAR-T (n=29): ORR 52% (CR 31%).
    • Early POD in DLBCL: 83%.
  • R/R FL (n=24, all Mosunetuzumab):
    • ORR: 91%.
    • CR Rate: 83%.
    • Early POD in FL: 25%.

Post-bsAb Relapse & CD20 Status:

  • 38 pts (46%) relapsed or did not respond.
  • New biopsy in 22/38 (59%): CD20 negativity in 19/22 (75% of re-biopsied; DLBCL n=16, FL n=3).

Genomic Analysis (49 pre-bsAb biopsies: 32 DLBCL, 17 FL):

  • Median 17 somatic driver mutations identified.
  • DLBCL Cohort (n=32):
    • Most frequent: KMT2D (43%), PIM1 (34%), TP53 (31%), BCL2 (28%), HLA-A (25%), MYD88 (22%), CD79B (19%), DTX1 (19%).
    • Mutations only in responders (CR/PR, n=16): SGK1 (4/16), ARID1A (2/16).
    • Mutations only in non-responders (n=16): EZH2 (2/16).
  • FL Cohort (n=17):
    • Most frequent: KMT2D (67%), BCL2 (61%), CREBBP (61%), TNFRSF14 (39%), ARID1A (35%), EZH2 (22%).
    • Comparison between responders/non-responders not testable (only 3 non-CR).

Summary & Conclusion

  • This real-world data analysis demonstrates the efficacy of commercially available bispecific antibodies in heavily pretreated R/R B-NHL patients, including those post-CAR-T.
  • High rates of CD20 negativity were observed in biopsies at relapse post-bsAb therapy.
  • In DLBCL, mutations in SGK1 and ARID1A were exclusively found in patients achieving CR or PR, while EZH2 mutations were only present in non-responding patients, suggesting potential biomarkers.
  • These findings suggest that bsAb therapies may mitigate the negative effects of several high-risk mutations. Further validation in larger cohorts is needed.
