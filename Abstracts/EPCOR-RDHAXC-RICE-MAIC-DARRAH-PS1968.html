PS1968 - Match-adjusted comparative analysis of Epcoritamab + R-DHAX/C or R-ICE vs R-DHAX/C or R-ICE in 2L+ transplant-eligible patients with diffuse large B-cell lymphoma

Justin Darrah, Ke Yang, Tongsheng Wang, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PS1968 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Subcutaneous epcoritamab (epcor), a CD3xCD20 T-cell engaging bispecific antibody, shows sustained efficacy and manageable safety in highly refractory B-cell lymphomas.
  • In R/R transplant-eligible DLBCL, epcor + salvage CIT (R-DHAX/C or R-ICE) in EPCORE NHL-2 (Arms 4 & 10) showed high response rates, enabling most pts to proceed to ASCT in the 2L+ setting.

Aims

To contextualize the benefit of adding epcoritamab to R-DHAX/C or R-ICE versus these CIT regimens alone in R/R DLBCL patients intended for transplant, in the absence of head-to-head trial data, using a match-adjusted indirect comparison (MAIC).

Methods & Study Design

  • Data Sources:
    • Epcor + R-DHAX/C or R-ICE: Individual patient data (IPD) from Arms 4 & 10 of EPCORE NHL-2 (N=63). Data cutoffs: Arm 4 (Jan 2024), Arm 10 (Dec 2024).
    • R-DHAX/C or R-ICE alone: IPD from COTA database (N=48). Observation period: Jan 2010 - Sep 2024.
  • Statistical Method: Inverse Probability of Treatment Weighting (IPTW) to balance cohorts across key baseline characteristics (age, gender, ECOG PS, COO, LDH, BM involvement, primary refractory status).
  • Outcomes: CR rate, ORR, PFS, OS. Subgroup analysis for 2L setting.
  • Analysis Models: Weighted log-binomial regression (for RR) and Cox regression models (for HR).

Baseline Characteristics (Pre-Adjustment)

Compared to CIT alone (N=48), Epcor+CIT (N=63) had more pts aged ≥65 (30.2% vs 25.0%), males (68.3% vs 56.3%), GCB subtype (50.8% vs 27.1%), Stage III/IV (69.8% vs 64.6%), and lower primary refractory status (57.1% vs 81.3%). ECOG PS 1-2 comparable (58.7% vs 62.5%). Cohorts were balanced after match-adjustment.

Efficacy Results (Match-Adjusted Comparison)

Median follow-up: Epcor+CIT 12.4 mo; CIT alone 9.3 mo.

  • Complete Response (CR) Rate:
    • Epcor + CIT: 62.8% (95% CI: 50.9-74.6)
    • CIT alone: 21.4% (95% CI: 9.6-33.1)
    • Relative Risk (RR): 2.94 (P<0.001)
  • Overall Response Rate (ORR):
    • Epcor + CIT: 82.0% (95% CI: 72.6-91.4)
    • CIT alone: 57.4% (95% CI: 43.2-71.6)
    • Relative Risk (RR): 1.43 (P=0.011)
  • Progression-Free Survival (PFS):
    • Epcor + CIT showed 83% reduction in risk of disease progression event.
    • Hazard Ratio (HR): 0.17 (95% CI: 0.09-0.32; P<0.001).
  • Overall Survival (OS):
    • Epcor + CIT showed 92% reduction in risk of mortality.
    • Hazard Ratio (HR): 0.08 (95% CI: 0.03-0.19; P<0.001).
  • Consistent findings observed in subgroup analysis of pts with only 1 prior systemic therapy.

Summary & Conclusion

  • This match-adjusted indirect comparison suggests that adding epcoritamab to salvage chemoimmunotherapy (R-DHAX/C or R-ICE) provides a significant 2.9-fold increase in the proportion of complete responders eligible for consolidation.
  • Furthermore, Epcoritamab + CIT demonstrated significant improvements in survival outcomes (PFS and OS) compared to CIT alone in transplant-eligible R/R DLBCL patients.
  • These findings support the potential of Epcoritamab + CIT to optimize salvage therapy for this patient population.
