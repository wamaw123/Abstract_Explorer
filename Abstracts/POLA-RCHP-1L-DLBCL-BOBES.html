PB3252 - Polatuzumab-R-CHP in previously untreated diffuse large B-cell lymphoma. A retrospective study.

Alejandro Bobes, Laura Llorente, Virginia Pradillo, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PB3252 | Publication Only

Background

  • DLBCL is the most prevalent NHL; R-CHOP was the established treatment.
  • Polatuzumab Vedotin (Pola), an anti-CD79b ADC, with R-CHP (Pola-RCHP) approved for 1L DLBCL based on POLARIX trial.
  • Limited experience with Polatuzumab in daily practice.

Aims

To assess effectiveness (response rates), safety (CTCAE NCI v5.0), and follow-up outcomes of Pola-RCHP in previously untreated DLBCL.

Methods & Study Design

  • Retrospective, multicenter (9 Spanish centers), descriptive study (15-MDA-LNH-2023).
  • Enrollment Period: Oct 2023 - Jan 2025.
  • Inclusion: >18 years, diagnosed DLBCL, treated with 1L Pola-RCHP (outside clinical trials).
  • Response Assessment: PET-CT (IWG 2007 criteria).
  • Toxicity: CTCAE v5.0. Analysis: SPSS.

Safety Summary (N=38)

  • No treatment-related deaths.
  • One dose reduction (due to recurrent febrile neutropenia).
  • GCSF for primary prophylaxis: 86% of patients.
  • Cycle delays: 19/211 cycles (9%), primarily due to neutropenia.
  • Most Common Side Effects (% of patients):
    • Febrile Neutropenia: 21%
    • Anemia: 10%
    • Respiratory Infections: 10%
    • Peripheral Neuropathy: 8%

Summary & Conclusion

  • This real-world study supports the effectiveness and safety of Pola-RCHP observed in the POLARIX phase 3 trial.
  • The study is ongoing for further expansion and continued follow-up.
