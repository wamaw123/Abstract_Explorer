Visual Summary: PB3323 - Pola-RCHP in 1L DLBCL with Hemophagocytic Lymphohistiocytosis (HLH)

Population & Aim (N=4)

Newly diagnosed DLBCL with HLH. Aim: Evaluate Pola-RCHP safety & efficacy.

Intervention

Pola-RCHP (Polatuzumab, Rituximab, Cyclophosphamide, Doxorubicin, Prednisone) x6 cycles, then Rituximab maintenance x2.

Key Efficacy (2/4 Pts Fully Evaluable)

ORR: 100% (Both CR at C8). HLH markers normalized in all 4 Pts.

Safety & Conclusion

Good tolerability. G4 neutropenia (1 Pt). Effective HLH control & DLBCL remission. Promising short-term outcomes.

PB3323 - A single-center pilot study of Pola-RCHP for treating newly diagnosed diffuse large B-cell lymphoma with hemophagocytic lymphohistiocytosis

Jianai Sun, Xiaolong Zheng, Jie Jin, Hongyan Tong, Wanzhuo Xie, De Zhou

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PB3323 | Publication Only

Background

  • Hemophagocytic lymphohistiocytosis (HLH) is a cytokine-mediated hyperinflammatory syndrome.
  • A subset of diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) patients presents with HLH initially, often associated with poor prognosis.
  • Pola-RCHP (polatuzumab vedotin + R-CHP) has shown significant efficacy and favorable safety in newly diagnosed DLBCL.

Aims

To evaluate the safety and therapeutic efficacy of Pola-RCHP in newly diagnosed DLBCL patients complicated by HLH.

Methods & Study Design

  • Single-center pilot study.
  • Enrollment: August 1, 2024 - December 31, 2025.
  • Patients (N=4): DLBCL presenting with HLH, meeting ≥5 HLH-2004 criteria. DLBCL diagnosed after HLH treatment.
  • HLH pre-treatment: Corticosteroids (2 Pts), Ruxolitinib + Corticosteroids (2 Pts).
  • Response assessment: 2014 Lugano criteria. Adverse Events (AEs): CTCAE v5.0.

Pola-RCHP Treatment Regimen

Eligible Patients (N=4) Newly Diagnosed DLBCL with HLH
▼
Pola-RCHP (q21d x 6 cycles) Polatuzumab vedotin: 1.8 mg/kg IV (D2) Rituximab: 375 mg/m² IV (D1) Cyclophosphamide: 750 mg/m² IV (D2) Doxorubicin: 150 mg/m² IV (D2) Prednisone: 100 mg PO (D2-6)
▼
Maintenance (x2 cycles) Rituximab monotherapy
Response (Lugano 2014) Safety (CTCAE v5.0) HLH Markers

Median follow-up: 150.5 days (range, 47-197).

Efficacy Results (N=4)

  • For 2 Pts who completed 8 cycles (6 Pola-RCHP + 2 R-mono):
    • PET-CT at 4th cycle: Both Partial Remission (PR).
    • PET-CT at 8th cycle: Both Complete Remission (CR).
  • For 2 Pts who completed 2-3 cycles:
    • HLH-related markers normalized.
    • No evidence of tumor on ultrasound or bone marrow aspiration (comprehensive PET-CT assessment pending).
  • Platelet counts normalized within 3-14 days.
  • Ferritin levels returned to normal (<500 ng/mL) after 1-4 cycles.

Safety Summary (N=4)

  • Regimen was generally well-tolerated.
  • One patient experienced Grade 4 neutropenia with fever and oropharyngeal Candida infection, which improved with colony-stimulating factor and antimicrobial therapy.
  • No Grade 4 thrombocytopenia or bleeding events were observed.

Summary & Conclusion

  • Pola-RCHP regimen exhibited a high response rate and promising short-term survival outcomes in patients with HLH-onset DLBCL.
  • It effectively achieved rapid control of HLH-related symptoms and induced deep remission of DLBCL.
  • The regimen demonstrated good tolerability.
  • Further studies with larger cohorts are warranted to validate the long-term efficacy and safety.
