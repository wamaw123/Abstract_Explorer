PB3294 - Pola-R-CHP in 1st line treatment DLBCL. Kuwait Cancer Center experience

Rasha Abd El-Tawab Hamed, Salem Alshemmari, Abdulaziz Hamadah, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PB3294 | Publication Only

Background

  • DLBCL: ~30% of NHL, most common aggressive lymphoma.
  • Incidence increases with age (most diagnosed 65-74 yrs).
  • ~40% relapse or are refractory to 1L R-CHOP. Median survival 6 mo if untreated.
  • Pola-R-CHP is an approved 1L treatment option.

Aims

To compare response to Pola-R-CHP (Polarix regimen) and 1-year PFS in newly diagnosed DLBCL patients at Kuwait Cancer Control Center (KCCC) with the original POLARIX trial.

Methods & Study Design

  • Retrospective data from KCCC. Patients treated since March 2023.
  • N=25 patients received Polarix regimen; 5 still on treatment.
  • Efficacy data presented for N=20 evaluable patients.

Safety Summary

Safety data (adverse events) were not detailed in this abstract, but outcomes were described as comparable to POLARIX.

Summary & Conclusion

Clinical Implications:

  • KCCC experience reinforces the applicability of Pola-R-CHP (Polarix regimen) even in patients with worse prognostic factors.
  • Promising results highlight potential effectiveness in real-world settings.
  • Data must be interpreted with caution due to small sample size (N=20 evaluable) and need for longer follow-up.
  • Further validation needed for similar real-world populations.
  • Extended follow-up of KCCC cohort and larger-scale studies in Gulf populations are needed.
