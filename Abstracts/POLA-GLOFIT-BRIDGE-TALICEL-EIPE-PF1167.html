N=14 R/R BCL Pts (≥15y) for Tali-cel CAR-T. Aim: Evaluate safety & efficacy of Pola-Glofit bridging therapy (BT).

Intervention (Pola-Glofit Bridging)

C1: Obi/R D1, Pola D2, Glofit D8(2.5mg)/D15(10mg). Subseq: Pola+Glofit(10mg) D1. Then Tali-cel CAR-T (Flu-Cy LD).

PF1167 - Safety and efficacy of Polatuzumab vedotin plus Glofitamab as bridging therapy prior to Talicabtagene autoleucel for relapsed/ refractory B-cell lymphoma

Thomas Eipe, Alok Shetty, Manju Sengar, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PF1167 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Talicabtagene autoleucel (Tali-cel), an anti-CD19 CAR-T therapy, is standard of care for R/R B-cell lymphomas (BCL) in India.
  • Most patients (pts) receive bridging therapy (BT), conventionally dexamethasone or radiation. Recent studies explore combination chemo and targeted agents.
  • A Phase Ib/II study showed complementary benefit of Glofitamab (Glofit) + Polatuzumab Vedotin (Pola) in R/R DLBCL with acceptable safety and good efficacy.
  • The impact of specific BT regimens like Pola-Glofit is poorly characterized.

Aims

To evaluate the safety and efficacy of using Polatuzumab Vedotin + Glofitamab (Pola-Glofit) as bridging therapy in R/R BCL patients undergoing anti-CD19 CAR-T cell therapy (Tali-cel).

Methods & Study Design

  • Study Type: Single-center, retrospective, real-world analysis.
  • Patient Population: R/R BCL pts (≥15 years) who received Pola-Glofit as BT prior to Tali-cel (Nov 2023 - Jan 2025).
  • Pola-Glofit Bridging Regimen (21-day cycles):
    • Cycle 1: Obinutuzumab (1000mg) or Rituximab (375mg/m²) D1, Pola (1.8mg/kg) D2, Glofit D8 (2.5mg) & D15 (10mg).
    • Subsequent Cycles: Pola (1.8mg/kg) D1, Glofit (10mg) D1.
  • CAR-T Therapy: Single infusion of Tali-cel (target dose ≥5x10⁶/kg) after Flu-Cy conditioning. Post CAR-T maintenance allowed.
  • Data Cutoff: January 30, 2025.

Patient Characteristics (Pola-Glofit Cohort, N=14)

  • Median Age: 55 years (range, 21-72).
  • Female: 57%.
  • Median Prior Lines of Therapy: 2 (range, 1-5).
  • Bulky Disease: 50%.
  • Bone Marrow Involvement: 21%.
  • Median Bridging Therapy Cycles: 1 (range, 1-3).

Efficacy Results

Post-Bridging Therapy (Pola-Glofit, N=14):

  • ORR: 64% (9/14).
  • CR: 14% (2/14).
  • SD: 1 patient.
  • PD: 4 patients (2 died before CAR-T).

Post Tali-cel Infusion (N=12 infused):

  • ORR: 75% (9/12).
  • CR: 58% (7/12).
  • Patients with PD post-BT did not respond to Tali-cel.
  • 2 deaths post Tali-cel (1 sepsis, 1 ICANS G2 with unresolved infection).

Survival (Median Follow-up: 7.8 months):

  • Median PFS: 5.7 months (95% CI, 2.2 - 9.2).
  • Median OS: 6.6 months (95% CI - Not Reached).
  • 6/9 responders remain disease-free with ongoing B-cell aplasia at data cutoff.

Safety Summary

Post-Bridging Therapy (Pola-Glofit, N=14):

  • Most common AE: CRS (36%; Grade 1: 29%, Grade 2: 7%).
  • No neurological events observed.
  • All CRS managed with tocilizumab and supportive care.
  • No ICU admissions or treatment-related deaths post-BT.

Post Tali-cel Infusion (N=12):

  • Grade 3-4 Cytopenias: All patients.
  • CRS: Grade 3-4 in 8% (1/12); Grade 1-2 in 50% (6/12).
  • ICANS: Grade 2 in 1 patient.
  • IEC-HS: 25% (3/12).
  • Hypogammaglobulinemia: 50% (6/12).

Summary & Conclusion

  • Pola-Glofit as bridging therapy in R/R BCL demonstrated high response rates (ORR 64%, CR 14%), even after a single cycle.
  • These responses were often sustained following Tali-cel CAR-T infusion (Post-CAR-T ORR 75%, CR 58%).
  • The Pola-Glofit combination showed a favorable safety profile with manageable toxicity as bridging therapy.
  • This approach appears promising for patients awaiting CAR-T cell therapy.
