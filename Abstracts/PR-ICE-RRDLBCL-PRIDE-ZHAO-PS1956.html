PS1956 - Interim analysis of Pomalidomide plus Rituximab, Ifosfamide, Carboplatin, and Etoposide (PR-ICE) in relapsed/refractory diffuse large B-cell lymphoma: A Phase I/II study (PRIDE Study)

Pengpeng Xu, Yang He, Yiwen Cao, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PS1956 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Relapsed/refractory diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (R/R DLBCL) has limited treatment options and poor prognosis.
  • Combining lenalidomide with R-ICE has shown promising results.
  • Pomalidomide (Pom), a third-generation immunomodulatory agent, has shown activity in R/R primary central nervous system lymphoma (PCNSL).

Aims

This Phase I/II study (PRIDE Study, ChiCTR2100049327) evaluates Pomalidomide + R-ICE (PR-ICE) as salvage for first-relapse or primary refractory DLBCL. This is an interim analysis.

Methods & Study Design

  • Patient Population: DLBCL pts with only one prior line of therapy, experiencing progression or no remission within 90 days post last dose.
  • Phase I (Dose Escalation, 3+3 design, N=9 planned):
    • R-ICE Q21D + Pomalidomide (2mg, 3mg, or 4mg daily, D1-10).
    • Primary Goal: Determine MTD or recommended dose based on DLTs.
  • Phase II (N=44 planned):
    • Pomalidomide at MTD + R-ICE.
    • Primary Endpoint: CR rate.
    • Secondary Endpoints: ORR, 2-year PFS, 2-year OS, safety.
  • Efficacy Assessment: After 2 and 3 treatment cycles.
  • Data Cutoff: February 14, 2025.

Patient Characteristics (N=36 Enrolled)

  • Median Age: 61 years.
  • ECOG Score 0-1: 33 patients.
  • Phase I Dose Escalation: 7 patients treated (3 at 2mg Pom, 4 at 3mg Pom).

Efficacy & MTD Results (Interim)

MTD Determination (Phase I):

  • DLTs in 3mg cohort: Grade 4 thrombocytopenia, Grade 4 rash.
  • MTD of Pomalidomide: 2 mg daily on days 1-10 with R-ICE.

Overall Efficacy (N=31 evaluable):

  • Overall Response Rate (ORR): 70.9% (22/31)
  • Complete Response (CR) Rate: 54.8% (17/31)
  • Partial Response (PR): 5 patients
  • Stable Disease (SD): 4 patients
  • Progressive Disease (PD): 5 patients

Efficacy for Pts Completing 3 Cycles (N=16):

  • CR Rate: 87.5% (14/16).
  • Subtypes:
    • MCD subtype (n=5): CR Rate 100%.
    • Other subtypes (n=7): CR Rate 80%.

Safety Summary (N=36)

  • Grade ≥3 Adverse Events (AEs): 61.1% of patients.
  • Most Common AEs (any grade, specific rates not provided for all):
    • Thrombocytopenia
    • Neutropenia
    • Leukopenia

Summary & Conclusion

  • The PR-ICE regimen (Pomalidomide 2mg MTD + R-ICE) demonstrated high efficacy in this interim analysis for patients with R/R DLBCL after one prior line of therapy.
  • CR rates were notably high (54.8% overall evaluable, 87.5% for those completing 3 cycles).
  • The safety profile was acceptable, with manageable hematological toxicities being the most common Grade ≥3 AEs.
  • These findings support further investigation of PR-ICE as a salvage therapy for R/R DLBCL.
