PB3280 - First-line treatment with Polatuzumab Vedotin improves efficacy and survival in patients with newly diagnosed advanced diffuse large B-cell lymphoma: A multicenter retrospective analysis

Sijun Bai, Xuliang Shen, Weiwei Tian, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PB3280 | Publication Only

Background

  • Polatuzumab Vedotin (Pola) has shown efficacy in R/R DLBCL.
  • Approved for 1L DLBCL in China based on POLARIX trial.
  • Limited real-world studies, especially comparing Pola-based vs conventional chemotherapy.

Aims

To provide evidence that Pola-based therapy improves efficacy and survival in patients with newly diagnosed advanced DLBCL in clinical practice.

Methods & Study Design

  • Multicenter (5 centers, China) retrospective study.
  • Enrollment: April 1, 2023 - Nov 1, 2024.
  • Patients: Newly diagnosed, advanced, high-risk, or bulky DLBCL.
    • Pola Group (N=35): Pola + chemotherapy.
    • Control Group (N=17): Conventional chemotherapy.
  • Primary Endpoint: ORR at end of treatment.
  • Secondary Endpoints: Median OS, PFS, DOR (Median F/U: 12.5 months).

Efficacy Results (Median F/U: 12.5 months)

Response after 4 Cycles:

  • Pola Group ORR: 100%
  • Conventional Chemo ORR: 75%
  • P-value: 0.018

Survival (Overall Cohort):

  • Median OS, PFS, DOR: Not reached in either group.

Survival Analysis (Patients with Advanced Disease):

  • OS: No significant difference (P=0.065)
  • PFS: Significant difference in favor of Pola (P=0.022)
  • DOR: Significant difference in favor of Pola (P=0.013)

Survival Analysis (Patients >60 years with Advanced Disease):

  • OS: No significant difference (P=0.083)
  • PFS: Significant difference in favor of Pola (P=0.014)
  • DOR: Significant difference in favor of Pola (P=0.015)

Pola Group Sub-analysis:

  • Pola-based therapy significantly enhanced PFS and DOR in advanced disease.
  • Appears insufficient in overcoming unfavorable prognosis of high P53 expression.

Safety Summary

Specific adverse event details were not provided in this abstract, but the conclusion mentions Pola-R-CHP is "well-tolerated".

Summary & Conclusion

  • Pola combination chemotherapy achieved better efficacy and prolonged survival (PFS, DOR) in newly diagnosed advanced DLBCL, including older patients.
  • Compared to R-CHOP, Pola-R-CHP is well-tolerated and shows promising efficacy.
  • Efficacy of Pola-based therapy in improving prognosis for patients with high P53 expression remains limited; further research needed for this subgroup.
