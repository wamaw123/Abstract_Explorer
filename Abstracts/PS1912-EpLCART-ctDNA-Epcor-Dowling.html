PS1912 - Circulating tumour DNA-directed intervention with Epcoritamab alone or in combination with Lenalidomide and Rituximab is feasible in the early post-CAR-T population at high risk of relapse: Preliminary data from EpLCART

Mark Dowling, Shafqat Inam, Hamish Scott, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PS1912 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Half or more patients treated with anti-CD19 CAR T-cell therapy for R/R LBCL do not experience durable remissions.
  • Monitoring ctDNA for MRD has prognostic value in LBCL post-CAR T.
  • Patients with MRD+ status post-CAR T (despite radiologic response) are at high risk of progression.
  • CD20xCD3 BsAbs can induce durable remissions in patients post-CAR T progression.

Aims

To hypothesize that BsAb (epcoritamab) may prevent progression in MRD positive patients post-CAR T, and that a low-burden disease state may lead to favorable disease control and safety with fixed-course BsAb. This abstract reports preliminary results for the first 10 patients in the EpLCART study.

Methods & Study Design

  • Trial: EpLCART (Phase II, multi-centre, NCT06414148).
  • Patient Population: LBCL pts post-commercial anti-CD19 CAR T-cell therapy, achieving PR/CMR but MRD+ between D+25 and D+100 post-infusion. ECOG ≤2, resolved CAR-T toxicities.
  • Intervention: 6 cycles of epcoritamab-alone or epcoritamab + lenalidomide + rituximab (epcor-R2).
  • ctDNA Monitoring: KAPA HyperCap DS NHL panel, ctDNAtools modified workflow.
  • Response Assessment: Interim PET/ctDNA after C2, EOT response after C6.
  • Primary Endpoint: CR by Lugano criteria at 12 months post CAR T-cell infusion.

EpLCART Study Design (MRD-Directed Consolidation)

LBCL Pts Post CD19 CAR-T PR/CMR by PET, MRD+ by ctDNA (D+25 to D+100)
▼
Intervention (6 Cycles) Epcoritamab-alone OR Epcoritamab + Lenalidomide + Rituximab (Epcor-R2)
Interim PET/ctDNA (Post C2) EOT PET/ctDNA (Post C6) Primary EP: CR at 12mo post CAR-T Safety (CRS, ICANS, Cytopenias)

Patient Enrollment & Baseline (N=10)

  • 42 patients underwent ctDNA testing post CAR-T (Feb 5 - Nov 4, 2024).
  • Median ctDNA turn-around time: 12 days.
  • 14 patients (33%) were MRD positive between D+25 and D+100.
  • Of MRD+ pts: 3 had PD (ineligible), 1 declined.
  • 10 patients enrolled in EpLCART.
  • (Detailed baseline characteristics of the 10 enrolled patients not provided in abstract text).

Preliminary Results (N=10)

Safety:

  • Treatment was well-tolerated.
  • No Grade >1 CRS.
  • No ICANS.
  • Grade 3-4 Neutropenia: 6 patients (60%).
  • Grade 3-4 Thrombocytopenia: 4 patients (40%).
  • Clinical or microbiologically proven infections: 9.

Efficacy (Interim Response Assessment Post Cycle 2):

  • MRD Negative: 8 out of 10 patients (80%).

Summary & Conclusion

  • Monitoring of ctDNA after CAR-T cell infusion is feasible, with a turn-around time allowing intervention prior to clinical progression in most cases.
  • Epcoritamab-alone or Epcoritamab-R2 appears deliverable in the early post-CAR-T period.
  • The treatment demonstrates a favorable toxicity profile with encouraging MRD responses.
  • Enrollment is ongoing (target N=40), and the study is planned to open at six CAR T-cell centers in Australia.
