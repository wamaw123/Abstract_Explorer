PF999 - Longitudinal targeted ctDNA analysis to assess CD20 and BAFF mutations in patients (pts) with relapsed/refractory (R/R) FL or DLBCL progressing on Odronextamab treatment in the ELM-2 study

David Tucker, Jon E. Arnason, Stefano Luminari, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PF999 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Odronextamab (CD20×CD3 bispecific Ab) showed compelling efficacy (ORR FL 80%, DLBCL 52%) and manageable safety in R/R FL/DLBCL (ELM-2 study, NCT03888105).
  • All patients (pts) had prior anti-CD20 antibody Tx and were CAR T-cell therapy naïve.
  • Undetectable Minimal Residual Disease (MRD) at Week 12 (W12) with odronextamab was associated with PFS benefit.

Aims

To identify mutations associated with progressive disease (PD) in adults with R/R Follicular Lymphoma (FL Grade 1-3a) or Diffuse Large B-Cell Lymphoma (DLBCL) treated with odronextamab in the ELM-2 study, using longitudinal ctDNA analysis.

Methods & Study Design

  • Study: Phase 2 ELM-2 (NCT03888105).
  • Patients: R/R FL or DLBCL receiving IV odronextamab until PD or unacceptable toxicity.
  • Sample Collection: Plasma for ctDNA at baseline (BL), on Tx (W12 ± 7 days), and at PET-CT (Q12W for CR pts). Matched BL tumor biopsies (≤180 days pre-Tx) if available.
  • Sequencing:
    • ctDNA: CAPP-Seq with a targeted immune resistance panel (8 B-cell target genes: CD20, CD19, CD22, CD58, CD79B, BAFF, BAFFR, BCMA).
    • Tumor Biopsies: Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS).
  • Data Cutoff: Aug 15, 2024.

Results: ctDNA Sequencing & Mutation Analysis

155 ctDNA samples from 121 pts sequenced. 101 pts had PD (FL n=20; DLBCL n=39) or were MRD+ at W12 without PD (FL n=15; DLBCL n=27). Control: 20 MRD- pts (BL ctDNA sequenced).

Baseline (BL) ctDNA Mutations (N=107 pts):

  • Somatic mutations detected in all 8 genes tested.
  • CD20: 7.5% (FL n=6; DLBCL n=2).
  • CD19: 11.2%.
  • CD22: 14.0%.
  • CD58: 8.4%.
  • CD79B: 15.9%.
  • BAFF: 6.5%.
  • BAFFR: 8.4%.
  • BCMA: 3.7%.
  • BL CD20 mutations did not appear to predict response to odronextamab.

Comparison of BL ctDNA vs. Tumor Biopsy (N=49 pts with biopsies):

  • Of 57 mutations in BL ctDNA, only 15 (26.3%) were also in tumor biopsies.
  • No BL tumor mutations detected in CD22, BAFF, BAFFR, or BCMA.

On-Treatment (W12) Mutations in Pts with PD and/or MRD+ (N=43):

  • CD20 Mutations: 7 pts (16.3%; FL n=3, DLBCL n=4).
    • 5/7 had p.S129F (predicted to impair CD20 expression).
    • Not detected at BL in 4/7 (3 had no BL sample).
    • PD in 4/7 with detectable CD20 mutation around progression.
  • BAFF Mutations: 14 pts (32.6%; FL n=5, DLBCL n=9).
    • 13/14 had p.E6K (predicted non-deleterious, potentially activating).
    • Not detected at BL in 9/14 (5 had no BL sample).
    • PD in 7/14 with detectable BAFF mutation around progression.
  • One FL pt in CR for ≥1 year had both CD20 p.S129F and BAFF p.E6K mutations at BL.

Summary & Conclusion

  • This analysis suggests that disease progression in pts with R/R FL or DLBCL treated with odronextamab could be partially attributable to treatment-emergent CD20 and BAFF mutations.
  • Minimally invasive ctDNA analysis (CAPP-Seq) showed utility beyond MRD determination, detecting more mutations than WGS from tumor biopsies, possibly due to greater sequencing depth.
  • Emergence of specific CD20 (e.g., p.S129F) and BAFF (e.g., p.E6K) mutations on treatment was observed in a subset of patients with PD or MRD positivity.
  • Ongoing analyses of ctDNA samples at earlier timepoints will help determine whether CD20/BAFF variant detection before PD can predict clinical outcomes.
