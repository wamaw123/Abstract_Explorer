PB3231 - First-line Selinexor in combination with R-CHOP for EBV-positive diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (XPLORE): A single-arm, phase Ib/II trial

Qingqing Cai, Rong Tao, Yi Xia, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PB3231 | Publication Only

Background

  • DLBCL is genetically and clinically heterogeneous.
  • Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) positivity (~8% of cases) is associated with inferior outcomes.
  • Selinexor (oral XPO1 inhibitor) showed preliminary efficacy with R-CHOP in NHL (Phase Ib).
  • XPLORE trial (NCT05577364): Single-arm, multicenter Phase Ib/II assessing Selinexor + R-CHOP in 1L EBV+ DLBCL.

Aims

To evaluate the safety and effectiveness of selinexor in combination with R-CHOP as first-line treatment in EBV-positive DLBCL patients.

Methods & Study Design

  • Eligible: 18-80 yrs, pathologically diagnosed DLBCL, EBER positivity (FISH), measurable disease.
  • Treatment Regimen:
    • R-CHOP: Standard doses q21d x 6 cycles.
    • Selinexor: Initiated from C2. Phase Ib "3+3" design: 40mg QW (C2) → 60mg QW (C3+), if no DLTs.
    • Post-Induction: Rituximab + Selinexor x 2 cycles.
    • Maintenance (for CR Pts after 8 cycles induction): Selinexor.
  • Primary Endpoints:
    • Phase Ib: MTD, RP2D.
    • Phase II: CR rate.
  • Data cutoff: Feb 14, 2025.

Efficacy Results (Median F/U: 12.6 months)

  • Patients completed median 7.5 combination treatment cycles (range 2-8).
  • RP2D of Selinexor: 60 mg once weekly. MTD not reached.
  • Evaluable Patients (N=16):
    • CR Rate: 75% (12/16)
    • ORR: 94% (15/16)

Summary & Conclusion

  • First-line Selinexor + R-CHOP demonstrated promising efficacy and an acceptable safety profile in EBV+ DLBCL.
  • Further monitoring of selinexor-related toxicities is needed.
  • The XPLORE trial is still recruiting, and evaluation is ongoing.
