Visual Summary: PF1318 - Spectral Flow Guided CAR-T Re-treatment in R/R B-cell Malignancies

Population & Aim

R/R B-cell malignancies (DLBCL, FL, MCL, MM; N=23) post-prior CAR-T. Aim: Evaluate spectral flow-guided CAR-T re-treatment targeting alternative antigens.

Methodology

Retrospective. Spectral flow cytometry profiled antigen expression (CD19, CD20, CD22, CD79b, BCMA, GPRC5D etc.) on relapsed tumors to guide CAR-T re-treatment target.

Key Efficacy (N=23)

ORR: 61%, CR: 46%. mDOR 7mo, mPFS 6mo. High target antigen expression correlated with better ORR (75% vs 40%).

Safety & Conclusion

Low G≥3 CRS (3%), no G≥3 ICANS, no TRM. Spectral flow-guided CAR-T re-treatment is feasible & effective for antigen escape.

PF1318: Spectral flow cytometry-guided selection of alternative tumor-associated membrane expression antigens for CAR-T cell re-treatment in relapsed/refractory B-cell malignancies

Yajing Zhang

EHA Library PF1318 | Abstract Release: 05/14/2025 | Presentation: 06/13/2025

Background

  • CAR-T cell therapy shows remarkable efficacy in R/R B-cell malignancies.
  • Disease relapse/progression is a significant challenge due to mechanisms like target antigen escape (loss/downregulation), CAR-T cell dysfunction, and an immunosuppressive TME.
  • Re-treatment with CAR-T cells against the original target is often ineffective, especially with antigen loss.
  • Identifying and targeting alternative antigens on residual malignant cells is a promising strategy.

Aims

To evaluate the feasibility and efficacy of CAR-T cell re-treatment, guided by comprehensive spectral flow cytometric profiling of target tumor-associated membrane surface antigen expression, in patients with R/R B-cell malignancies who have progressed after prior CAR-T therapy.

Methods & Study Design

  • Study Type: Retrospective analysis.
  • Patient Population: Patients with R/R B-cell malignancies who relapsed/progressed after prior CAR-T therapy.
  • Sample Analysis: Pre-re-treatment samples (bone marrow aspirate, peripheral blood, or tumor biopsy) analyzed by multi-parameter spectral flow cytometry.
  • Comprehensive Antibody Panel Used to Quantify:
    • Potential CAR-T Targets: CD19, CD20, CD79b, CD22, BCMA, CD38, CD30, CD70, GPRC5D, CS1, NKG2D, and others.
    • T-cell Exhaustion Markers: PD-1, TIM-3, LAG-3.
    • TME Components: T-cell subsets, NK cells, MDSCs, Tregs, TAMs.

Efficacy of Re-treatment (N=23)

Prior CAR-T targets: CD19 and BCMA.

Antigen Expression & Re-treatment Targets:

  • Substantial heterogeneity in antigen expression profiles post-initial CAR-T.
  • Post-CD19 CAR-T Relapse: 19% CD19 loss, 32% CD19 downregulation, 49% maintained CD19. Alternative targets (CD20, CD22, CD79b) frequently identified.
  • Post-BCMA CAR-T Relapse: 43% BCMA downregulation. Alternative targets (GPRC5D, CD38, CS1, NKG2D) observed.
  • Re-treatment CAR-T Targets Utilized: CD19/20 (n=3), CD20 (n=2), CD22 (n=5), CD79b (n=2), GPRC5D (n=5), CS1 (n=1), NKG2D (n=2), CD38 (n=3).

  • Median Duration of Response (DOR): 7 months (95% CI: 5-11)
  • Median Progression-Free Survival (PFS): 6 months (95% CI: 4-10)
  • Median Overall Survival (OS): Projected to exceed 1 year
  • Correlation: High target antigen expression (>67.7% of malignant cells) significantly correlated with higher ORR (75% vs. 40%, p = 0.02).

Safety Summary (N=23 Re-treatment)

  • Grade ≥3 Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS): 3%
  • Grade ≥3 Immune Effector Cell-Associated Neurotoxicity Syndrome (ICANS): 0%
  • Treatment-Related Mortality (TRM): 0%

The re-treatment strategy demonstrated a manageable safety profile.

Summary & Conclusion

  • Spectral flow cytometry-guided selection of alternative target antigens is a feasible and effective strategy in patients with R/R B-cell malignancies who have experienced failure of prior CAR-T therapy.
  • This personalized approach facilitates the identification of viable alternative targets, potentially mitigating the impact of antigen escape.
  • The study demonstrated promising clinical outcomes (ORR 61%, CR 46%) with manageable toxicity, supporting this strategy for CAR-T re-treatment.
  • High expression of the chosen alternative target antigen correlated with improved response rates.
