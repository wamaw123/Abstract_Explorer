PS2077 - Using PSM comparing the prognostic differences between first-line autologous transplantation and chemotherapy alone in newly diagnosed DLBCL patients with IPI ≥3

Hongmei Jing, Lan Ma, Shuozi Liu, Hui Liu, Jiefei Bai, Wenrong Huang, Junli Chen, Li Bao, Shan Gao, Wanling Sun, Zhenling Li, Min Wang

EHA2025 Congress | Abstract #PS2077 | Poster Presentation | Session: Stem cell transplantation - Clinical

Background

  • Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) is the most common aggressive B-cell lymphoma.
  • The International Prognostic Index (IPI) score ≥3 signifies a poor prognosis (intermediate-high or high risk).
  • For younger patients in these risk groups, the benefit of autologous hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (ASCT) as first-line consolidation is debated.

Aims

To compare whether ASCT after first-line treatment improved the prognosis of newly diagnosed DLBCL patients with IPI score ≥3, compared to chemotherapy alone, using propensity score matching (PSM) for consistent baseline characteristics.

Methods & Study Design

  • Retrospective analysis of patients from Beijing 6 medical center (March 2014 - March 2023).
  • Inclusion: Newly diagnosed DLBCL, IPI score ≥3.
  • Groups:
    • Transplant group (N=104 pre-PSM): Underwent ASCT after achieving remission with first-line treatment.
    • Non-transplant group (N=90 pre-PSM): Received only chemotherapy or chemotherapy combined with radiotherapy.
  • PSM: 1:1 matching based on age, resulting in 70 pairs (N=140 total) for analysis.
  • Endpoints: Progression-free survival (PFS), Overall Survival (OS).

Subgroup Analysis (PFS Benefit for Transplant Group)

Statistically significant PFS improvement with ASCT in:

  • IPI score 3 (HR = 0.305, P = 0.034)
  • Non-GCB subtype (HR = 0.279, P = 0.012)
  • LDH > 250 U/L (HR = 0.349, P = 0.019)
  • Double-expressor status (HR = 0.299, P = 0.036)
  • Bone involvement (HR = 0.281, P = 0.021)

Trends for OS benefit in IPI 3, non-GCB, and LDH > 250 U/L subgroups (P=0.080, P=0.081, P=0.054 respectively).

Safety

Safety details of ASCT or chemotherapy regimens were not detailed in this abstract.

Summary & Conclusion

  • ASCT appears to be an important therapeutic approach to improve PFS in specific subgroups of DLBCL patients with intermediate-high or high risk (IPI ≥3).
  • Potential benefits in OS warrant further investigation.
