PS1919 - Tirabrutinib for the treatment of relapsed or refractory primary central nervous system lymphoma: Efficacy and safety from the Phase II PROSPECT study

Lakshmi Nayak, Christian Grommes, Avyakta Kallam, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PS1919 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Primary Central Nervous System Lymphoma (PCNSL) is a rare, aggressive non-Hodgkin lymphoma localized to the CNS.
  • Treatment options are limited, standard of care is not well established, and prognosis is poor, especially in the relapsed/refractory (r/r) setting.
  • Tirabrutinib, a highly potent, selective second-generation Bruton’s tyrosine kinase (BTK) inhibitor, is approved in Japan, Taiwan, and South Korea for r/r PCNSL based on a phase I/II study in Japanese patients.
  • Currently, no approved drug therapies exist for PCNSL in the US or Europe.

Aims

To report the efficacy and safety results from the PROSPECT study (NCT04947319), an open-label, phase II study of tirabrutinib monotherapy in patients with r/r PCNSL conducted in the US.

Methods & Study Design

  • Study Type: Open-label, phase II (PROSPECT study - NCT04947319).
  • Patient Population: Patients with r/r PCNSL.
  • Intervention: Oral tirabrutinib 480 mg monotherapy once daily (QD) until disease progression (PD) or unacceptable toxicity.
  • Primary Endpoint: Overall Response Rate (ORR) assessed by Independent Review Committee (IRC).
  • Secondary Endpoints: Duration of Response (DOR), Time To Response (TTR), Best Overall Response (BOR), safety.
  • Exploratory Endpoints: Overall Survival (OS), Progression-Free Survival (PFS).
  • Data Cutoff: November 1, 2024.

Patient Characteristics (N=48)

  • Median Age: 65.5 years (range, 34-87).

Efficacy Results (Median Follow-up: 11.2 months)

  • Overall Response Rate (ORR): 66.7% (32/48)
    • Complete Response Rate (CRR; CR + CRu): 43.8% (21/48)
    • Partial Response Rate (PRR): 22.9% (11/48)
  • Median Duration of Response (mDOR): 9.3 months (range, 0.0-23.5).
  • Median Time To Response (mTTR): 0.95 months (range, 0.9-3.7).
  • Median Overall Survival (mOS): Not Reached (range, 1.0-33.0).
  • Median Progression-Free Survival (mPFS): 6.0 months (range, 0.0-26.0).

At data cutoff, 27.1% (n=13) of patients remained on tirabrutinib treatment.

Safety Summary (N=48)

  • Any-grade Treatment-Emergent Adverse Events (TEAEs): 97.9% (n=47).
  • Grade ≥3 TEAEs: 56.3% (n=27).
  • Any-grade Treatment-Related Adverse Events (TRAEs): 75.0% (n=36).
    • Most frequent TRAEs (any grade): Anemia (18.8%), Fatigue (14.6%), Neutrophil count decreased (14.6%), Pruritus (14.6%), Rash (14.6%), Maculo-papular rash (14.6%).
  • Grade ≥3 TRAEs: 27.1% (n=13).
    • Most frequent G≥3 TRAEs: Neutrophil count decreased (8.3%), Rash maculo-papular (4.2%).
  • Deaths related to TEAEs: 2 (4.2%) - seizure/pneumonia (n=1), fall (n=1); considered unrelated to study treatment.
  • Discontinuations due to AE: 1 patient (2.1%).
  • Main reasons for discontinuation: Disease progression (54.2%), death (8.3% - includes the 2 G5 TEAEs).

Summary & Conclusion

  • Tirabrutinib monotherapy demonstrated an Overall Response Rate (ORR) of 66.7% and a Complete Response (CR/CRu) rate of 43.8% in patients with r/r PCNSL.
  • The median Duration of Response (mDOR) was 9.3 months.
  • The safety profile was manageable, with 27.1% of patients experiencing Grade ≥3 treatment-related adverse events.
  • The PROSPECT trial results support tirabrutinib monotherapy as a potentially effective treatment option for patients with r/r PCNSL, a population with high unmet medical need.
