PF1285 - Exploring short-term patient-reported adverse events (AEs) and health-related quality of life (HRQoL) by CAR T-cell product in patients with aggressive B-cell lymphomas: Evidence from a real-world study by the GIMEMA

Fabio Efficace, A. Ghisolfi, L. Bracci, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PF1285 | Publication Only

Background

  • Chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) T-cell therapy has markedly transformed care for patients with aggressive B-cell lymphomas.
  • However, very limited evidence is available on patient-reported AEs and HRQoL.

Aims

  • To describe the prevalence of short-term (day+10) patient-reported AEs by the type of CAR T-cell product in patients treated in real-life.
  • To explore the prognostic value of the CAR T-cell product for short-term health-related quality of life (HRQoL).

Methods & Study Design

  • Study Type: Multicenter, prospective observational study (GIMEMA).
  • Patient Population: Adults with aggressive B-cell lymphomas scheduled to undergo CAR T-cell therapy.
  • Data Collection:
    • Symptomatic AEs assessed on day +10 post-infusion using 20 items from the NCI PRO-CTCAE Item Library (items included difficulty swallowing, decreased appetite, nausea, diarrhea, swelling, hair loss, sleepiness, concentration, memory, general pain, headache, muscle pain, joint pain, insomnia, fatigue, anxious, discouragement, sad, and chills).
    • HRQoL assessed with short-term (month 1) global health status/QoL scale of the EORTC QLQ-C30.
  • Analysis: Descriptive analyses for AEs. Multivariable linear regression to assess whether CAR T-cell product was independently associated with HRQoL, adjusting for baseline HRQoL, age, gender, ECOG PS, time from diagnosis to infusion, IELSG, comorbidities, etc.

Results

Patient-Reported Adverse Events (Day+10):

  • Patients infused with axi-cel were younger (median age 53y vs 64y) and had a better ECOG-PS.
  • No significant differences in AE prevalence based on CAR-T product, except for 'feeling sad' (p=0.03), which was highest in the brexu-cel and liso-cel group.

Health-Related Quality of Life (Month 1):

  • Multivariable analyses showed the type of CAR T-cell product was not a significant prognostic factor for short-term HRQoL.
  • Only baseline HRQoL (p<0.001) and IELSG score (p=0.03) were found to be independent prognostic factors for month 1 HRQoL.

Summary & Conclusion

  • This real-world study provides insights into the short-term patient-reported AEs and HRQoL across different CAR T-cell products.
  • Findings may help better understand the impact of CAR T-cell products on symptom prevalence and suggest that the type of CAR T-cell product is not associated with short-term HRQoL.
  • Further research is warranted to elucidate factors predicting patient-reported outcomes and HRQoL.
