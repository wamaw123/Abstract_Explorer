PS1991 - Assessment of circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) for response prediction and genetic characterization of relapsed/refractory large B-cell lymphoma (R/R LBCL) receiving Glofitamab treatment

Chiara Signori, Cristina Zucchinetti, Matin Salehi, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PS1991 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Glofitamab (CD20:CD3 bispecific antibody) is approved for R/R LBCL, showing high CR rates and durable responses in the pivotal Phase 2 trial (NCT03075696).
  • A subset of patients experiences relapse or progression.
  • ctDNA analysis can identify response biomarkers and resistance mechanisms.

Aims

To assess ctDNA to identify response biomarkers and resistance mechanisms to Glofitamab and optimize risk stratification for R/R LBCL patients.

Methods & Study Design

  • Patients: 57 R/R LBCL patients treated with Glofitamab (Phase 1-2 trial or Italian EAP).
  • Sample Collection: Blood samples at baseline, Cycle 3 (C3), Cycle 6, End of Treatment (EOT), or disease progression.
  • ctDNA Analysis: Plasma cell-free DNA sequenced using CAPP-Seq strategy. ctDNA load calculated as haploid genome equivalent per ml (hGE/ml).
  • Genomic Analysis: Copy number (CN) and fusion analyses (CNVkit, FACTERA). Non-negative Matrix Factorization (NMF) for genetic subtyping.

Results

Baseline ctDNA (N=57):

  • Median ctDNA load: 292 hGE/ml (range 27-14,804).
  • Higher ctDNA levels associated with bulky disease (p=0.02) and high IPI (p=0.03).

Longitudinal ctDNA (N=43 received â‰¥10mg Glofitamab):

  • At C3:
    • MRD positive (MRD+): 18/43 (42%). All progressed during treatment.
      • 5 cases were in metabolic CR/PR at C3 by PET.
    • MRD negative (MRD-): 25/43 (58%).
      • 19/25 experienced persistent CR.
      • 4/25 progressed after initial CR (with simultaneous MRD reappearance).
  • MRD- patients at C3 had significantly longer median PFS (11.8 vs 2.5 months; p < 0.0001).
  • Early MRD evaluation had better positive predictive value (PPV) for outcome than early PET (100% vs 85.7%).
  • ctDNA Kinetics: CR patients achieved rapid/sustained MRD negativity; non-CR patients had stable/increasing ctDNA.

Genetic Landscape (Baseline):

  • TP53 disruptions (mutations n=22, deletions n=4) vs. wild-type: Comparable CR rates (42% vs 36%) and median PFS (4.6 vs 4 months).
  • NMF Clustering (5 genetic subtypes):
    • Cluster 1 (14%, NF-kB alterations): Best outcome (57% CR).
    • Cluster 2 (31%, several CN alterations): 40% CR.
    • Cluster 4 (8%, epigenetic regulation genes): All patients progressed.

Safety Summary

Safety data was not the focus of this abstract and is not detailed.

Summary & Conclusion

  • Detection of C3 MRD in R/R LBCL patients receiving Glofitamab effectively predicts progression.
  • Longitudinal ctDNA assessment accurately reflects disease status.
  • Mutational analysis allows for deep characterization of the cohort's genetic profile and investigation of Glofitamab resistance mechanisms.
  • These findings highlight the potential role of ctDNA in disease monitoring and optimization of risk stratification in R/R LBCL patients.
