PF939: Overall survival improvement of the R/R DLBCL patient population treated in the year 2021 and beyond

Prokop Vodicka, Andrea Janíková, Robert Pytlík, Kamila Polgarova, Alice Sykorova, Frantisek Folber, Juraj Duras, Katerina Steinerova, Heidi Mociková, Vít Procházka, Katerina Benesova, David Belada, Michal Kascak, Jiří Šrámek, Flóra Benkő, Pavel Klener, Jitka Dlouhá, Petra Blahovcova, Marek Trneny

EHA Library PF939 | Abstract Release: 05/14/2025 | Presentation: 06/13/2025

Background

  • The treatment landscape for relapsed/refractory diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (R/R DLBCL) has evolved with novel therapies: CAR-T (for L3+, implemented 2020, generally available from 2021 in the region), bispecific antibodies, and ADCs.
  • While novel therapies improved EFS in L3+ registration trials (mostly single-arm Phase 2, except Pola-BR), their real-world impact on R/R DLBCL outcomes is unclear.

Aims

This analysis aimed to assess treatment shifts in L3+ for R/R DLBCL and evaluate whether novel therapies changed survival outcomes (OS as primary endpoint) in a real-world patient population.

Methods

  • Data Source: Prospective NiHiL registry (NCT03199066).
  • Patient Population: 312 consecutive R/R DLBCL patients, initially treated with R-CHOP-like regimens, receiving L3+ between 2018-2023.
  • Era Definition (based on L3 CAR-T access):
    • Era 1: 2018-2020
    • Era 2: 2021-2023
  • Endpoints:
    • Primary: Overall Survival (OS).
    • Secondary: Event-Free Survival (EFS - time from L3+ to next line, progression, death, or last follow-up), therapeutic shifts.
  • Subgroup Analysis: CAR-T-eligible patients (age ≤ 75 years, ECOG PS ≤ 2; n=202).

Summary & Conclusion

  • The adoption of novel therapies (including CAR-T and other innovative treatments) has substantially improved outcomes for R/R DLBCL patients treated with L3+.
  • Overall mortality was reduced by 26% (HR 0.74) in the whole cohort and by 31% (HR 0.69) in CAR-T-eligible patients.
  • The relative risk of an event (progression, next line, death) was reduced by 28% (HR 0.72) in the whole cohort and by 40% (HR 0.60) in CAR-T-eligible patients.
  • These findings highlight the significant real-world impact of CAR-T and other innovative therapies on survival in the overall R/R DLBCL population and support their use beyond clinical trials.
