PS1942 - Match-adjusted comparative analysis of the efficacy of Epcoritamab + R-miniCHOP vs R-miniCHOP in previously untreated diffuse large B-cell lymphoma

Justin Darrah, Juraj Duras, Tongsheng Wang, Guihua Zhang, Alex Mutebi, Vardhaman Patel, Anthony Wang, Mohammad Atiya, Malene Risum

EHA2025 Congress | Abstract #PS1942 | Poster Presentation | Session: Aggressive Non-Hodgkin lymphoma - Clinical

Background

  • Epcoritamab (epcor) is a subcutaneously administered CD3xCD20 bispecific antibody.
  • EPCORE NHL-2 (Arm 8) showed high response rates and compelling survival with epcor + R-miniCHOP in 1L DLBCL patients (pts) unable to tolerate standard R-CHOP.

Aims

To conduct a match-adjusted comparative analysis of efficacy outcomes in pts with 1L DLBCL treated with epcor + R-miniCHOP (EPCORE NHL-2) vs R-miniCHOP alone (COTA database), in the absence of head-to-head data.

Methods & Study Design

  • Data Sources:
    • Epcor + R-miniCHOP: Individual patient data (IPD) from Arm 8 of EPCORE NHL-2 (N=28; data cutoff: May 2024; MFU: 16.8 mo).
    • R-miniCHOP alone: IPD from COTA US electronic health records database (N=46; Jan 2019 - Mar 2024; MFU: 19.4 mo).
  • Statistical Method: Inverse Probability of Treatment Weighting (IPTW) to balance cohorts on key baseline characteristics (age, gender, ECOG PS, COO, LDH, Ann Arbor Stage, double/triple-hit, WBC).
  • Outcomes Compared: ORR, CR rate, PFS, OS.
  • Analysis Models: Weighted logistic regression (ORs for response) and Cox proportional-hazards models (HRs for time-to-event).

Patient Characteristics (Pre-Adjustment)

Epcor + R-miniCHOP pts (N=28) were older (≥75 years: 96.4% vs 69.6%), had higher LDH (64.3% vs 45.7%), more Stage III/IV (64.3% vs 47.8%), and higher WBC (21.4% vs 6.5%) vs R-miniCHOP pts (N=46). Cohorts balanced after MAIC.

Safety

Safety data for the individual arms (EPCORE NHL-2 and COTA) were not detailed in this MAIC-focused abstract. The abstract states EPCORE NHL-2 Arm 8 demonstrated a manageable safety profile in its primary publication.

Summary & Conclusion

  • This matched analysis suggests that combining epcoritamab with R-miniCHOP significantly increases the CR rate (by 38%) and reduces the risk of death by 80% compared with R-miniCHOP alone.
  • A 64% reduction in the risk of progression was also observed, trending towards statistical significance.
  • These benefits are observed despite small sample sizes and limited follow-up.
