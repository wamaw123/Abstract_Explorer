PB3264 - The CMOP±R regimen shows high efficacy as a first-line treatment for Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma: A phase II clinical trial with matching-adjusted indirect treatment comparison (MAIC) analysis

Tao You, Jingxian Gu, Peng Xu, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PB3264 | Publication Only

Background

  • NHL poses a challenge with high mortality; R/R disease common in high IPI patients.
  • CMOP (Cyclophosphamide, Mitoxantrone liposome, Vincristine, Prednisone) showed efficacy in newly diagnosed PTCL (Huang et al., ASH 2022).
  • Efficacy of CMOP±R in other NHL subtypes is uncertain.

Aims

To evaluate the efficacy and safety of the CMOP±R regimen as a first-line therapy for newly diagnosed NHL.

Methods & Study Design

  • Prospective, single-arm, open-label, multicenter phase 2 study (ChiCTR2300074073).
  • Patients: Adults with newly diagnosed NHL.
  • Intervention: CMOP±R regimen (Cyclophosphamide, Mitoxantrone hydrochloride liposome, Vincristine, Prednisone ± Rituximab) q4wks for max 6 cycles, or until PD/unacceptable toxicity.
  • Primary Endpoint: ORR after induction.
  • Secondary Endpoints: PFS, OS.
  • MAIC: Unanchored, weighted to match POLARIX trial baseline characteristics for comparison.
  • AEs: NCI-CTCAE v5.0.

Efficacy Results (Median F/U: 9.0 months)

Overall Population (N=31 evaluable for best response):

  • ORR: 96.8% (30/31)
  • CRR: 77.4% (24/31)
  • PD: 4 patients

Subgroup Analysis:

  • Advanced IPI (3-5): ORR 100%, CRR 87.5%
  • DLBCL (N=12): ORR 91.7% (11/12), CRR 83.3% (10/12)
  • PTCL (N=8): ORR 100% (8/8), CRR 75% (6/8)

Safety Summary (N=39)

Most common Grade 3-4 TRAEs:

  • Lymphopenia: 41.0%
  • Neutropenia: 35.9%
  • Leukopenia: 25.6%

Anemia (any grade): 51.3% (low G3+ incidence)

Thrombocytopenia (any grade): 33.3% (low G3+ incidence)

Cardiac: Normal LVEF (median 63.5%), normal CK-MB. No atrial fibrillation or severe cardiac arrhythmias.

Deaths: 4 observed (3 non-relapse mortality).

Summary & Conclusion

  • The CMOP±R regimen exhibited notable efficacy as a first-line treatment for NHL, with a manageable safety profile.
  • MAIC results suggest CMOP±R is associated with a favorable response in DLBCL patients compared to R-CHOP or Pola-R-CHP from POLARIX (though not statistically significant).
