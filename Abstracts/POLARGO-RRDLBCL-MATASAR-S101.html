S101 - Polatuzumab vedotin, rituximab, gemcitabine and oxaliplatin (Pola-R-GemOx) for relapsed/refractory (R/R) diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL): results from the randomized Phase III POLARGO trial

Matthew Matasar, Zhiming Li, Theodoros P. Vassilakopoulos, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #S101 | Oral Presentation

Background

  • Polatuzumab vedotin (Pola) is a CD79b-directed antibody-drug conjugate (ADC) that targets and kills B-cells.
  • Pola is approved for use in both first-line (Pola-R-CHP) and relapsed/refractory (R/R) DLBCL (Pola-BR).
  • The POLARGO trial (NCT04182204) was designed to evaluate the benefit of Pola-R-GemOx versus R-GemOx in transplant-ineligible R/R DLBCL patients.

Methods & Study Design

  • Phase III, randomized, open-label trial in patients with transplant-ineligible R/R DLBCL after at least one prior line of therapy.
  • The study included an initial safety run-in cohort (n=15) treated with Pola-R-GemOx.
  • In the randomized phase, patients were assigned 1:1 to receive either Pola-R-GemOx or R-GemOx every 3 weeks for up to 8 cycles.
  • Pola dose was 1.8 mg/kg.
  • Stratification factors included age, number of prior therapies, and relapsed vs refractory status.
  • Primary endpoint: Overall Survival (OS). Key secondary endpoints: Progression-Free Survival (PFS, by investigator), Complete Response (CR, by IRC), and Overall Response Rate (ORR, by IRC).

Efficacy Results (Median F/U: 24.6 months )

Response Rates by PET-CT at EOT (IRC-assessed)

  • Overall Survival (OS): Pola-R-GemOx significantly improved OS, with a median OS of 19.5 months vs 12.5 months for R-GemOx. The stratified HR was 0.60 (p=0.0017). Unstratified analysis showed an HR of 0.64 (95% CI: 0.47-0.89).
  • Progression-Free Survival (PFS): PFS was also significantly improved, with a median PFS of 7.4 months for Pola-R-GemOx vs 2.7 months for R-GemOx. The stratified HR was 0.37 (p<0.0001). Unstratified analysis showed an HR of 0.46 (95% CI: 0.34-0.62).
  • The survival benefit of Pola-R-GemOx was consistent across most subgroups, including both ABC and GCB cell-of-origin types.

Conclusion

In the Phase III POLARGO trial, Polatuzumab vedotin combined with R-GemOx significantly improved overall survival and progression-free survival compared with R-GemOx alone in transplant-ineligible patients with relapsed/refractory DLBCL. The safety profile was manageable and consistent with the known risks of the individual agents.
