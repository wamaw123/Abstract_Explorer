PB3326 - Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma - Single center experience in Bulgaria

Yanitsa Davidkova, Naum Simonoski, Tanya Yankova, Martin Donchev

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PB3326 | Publication Only

Background

  • The diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) is the most prevalent aggressive subtype of non-Hodgkin’s lymphoma.
  • For the last years standard of first line therapy was the regimen R-CHOP (rituximab, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine and prednisone).
  • However, according to the WHO classification DLBCL consists of heterogeneous entities based on molecular and prognostic characterization.
  • Therefore, diverse therapeutic options are emerging in order to enhance the clinical outcome.

Aims

The aim of the study was to evaluate newly diagnosed DLBCL patients treated with different therapeutic options R-CHOP, R-DA-EPOCH (dose adjusted etoposide, prednisone, vincristine, cyclophosphamide and doxorubicin) and pola-R-CHP (polatuzumab vedotin, rituximab, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, and prednisone).

Methods & Study Design

  • Single-center retrospective cohort study (National Hematology Hospital, Bulgaria).
  • Patients: 40 consecutive, previously untreated DLBCL patients over a 2-year period.
  • Diagnosis: Established according to the WHO classification criteria. Immunohistochemistry performed locally.
  • Primary Endpoints: Overall survival (OS) and progression-free survival (PFS).
  • Statistical Analysis: Pearson’s Chi-test, Kaplan-Meier survival curves.

Efficacy Results by Treatment Regimen

Patient Distribution by Regimen:

  • R-CHOP: N=18 (45%)
  • R-DA-EPOCH: N=15 (37.5%)
  • Pola-R-CHP: N=7 (17.5%)

Baseline Differences between Regimens:

  • Mean Age: R-DA-EPOCH significantly younger (37.9y) vs R-CHOP (54.6y) & Pola-R-CHP (47.6y) (p=0.003).
  • Bulky Disease: 71% in R-DA-EPOCH vs 28.6% in R-CHOP & 0% in Pola-R-CHP (p=0.002).
  • IPI High-Int/High: R-CHOP (80%) vs R-DA-EPOCH (67%) vs Pola-R-CHP (50%).

CR Rate after 4 Cycles:

  • R-CHOP: 59%
  • R-DA-EPOCH: 23.5%
  • Pola-R-CHP: 17.5%
  • (p=0.3, not significant)

Progression after 4 Cycles:

  • R-CHOP: 1 patient
  • R-DA-EPOCH: 3 patients
  • Pola-R-CHP: 1 patient

No significant difference in OS or PFS between the three regimens (p=0.4).

Safety Summary

Safety data (adverse events) were not detailed in this abstract text.

Summary & Conclusion

  • Because of limited sample size of the investigated cohort, further prospective studies are required in order to distinguish feasible prognostic markers related to therapeutic options.
