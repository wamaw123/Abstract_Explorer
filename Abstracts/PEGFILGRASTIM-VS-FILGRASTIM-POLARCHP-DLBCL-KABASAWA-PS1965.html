PS1965 - Pegfilgrastim vs. filgrastim for febrile neutropenia prevention in the first cycle of Polatuzumab Vedotin-R-CHP (Pola-R-CHP): a nationwide cohort study in newly diagnosed diffuse large B-cell lymphoma

Nobuyuki Kabasawa, Daisuke Takada, Tetsuji Morishita, Noriko Sasaki, Susumu Kunisawa, Yuichi Imanaka

EHA2025 Congress | Abstract #PS1965 | Poster Presentation | Session: Aggressive Non-Hodgkin lymphoma - Clinical

Background

  • The standard treatment for diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) has been well established.
  • The phase 3 POLARIX trial demonstrated that Pola-R-CHP improved progression-free survival compared to R-CHOP.
  • However, the incidence of febrile neutropenia (FN) was higher in the Pola-R-CHP group than in the R-CHOP group (14.3% vs. 8.0%).
  • No reports have clarified the optimal FN prevention strategy for Pola-R-CHP, including the choice of granulocyte colony-stimulating factor (G-CSF), its administration timing, and duration.

Aims

To compare the effectiveness of pegfilgrastim and filgrastim in preventing FN in the first cycle of Pola-R-CHP in newly diagnosed DLBCL patients.

Methods & Study Design

  • This retrospective cohort study utilized a Diagnosis Procedure Combination (DPC) database covering over 500 acute care hospitals in Japan.
  • The study included newly diagnosed, untreated DLBCL patients aged ≥18 years who were hospitalized for the first cycle of Pola-R-CHP between August 2022 and March 2024 and received either pegfilgrastim or filgrastim prophylactically.
  • An inverse probability of treatment weighting (IPTW) approach using propensity score analysis was performed to compare FN incidence, adjusting for age, sex, body mass index, cancer stage, Charlson comorbidity index, activities of daily living, initial dose ratio, and prophylactic antibiotic use.
  • Primary endpoint: Incidence of FN. FN was defined as cases requiring broad-spectrum antibiotics initiated on the same day as blood culture collection, from the day after chemotherapy initiation and within 21 days in patients who received G-CSF prophylaxis.
  • Secondary endpoints: In-hospital mortality and length of hospital stay. The study also assessed G-CSF administration timing and duration.

Patient Characteristics (N=310)

Among 773 DLBCL patients, 310 received G-CSF prophylaxis (pegfilgrastim: 141 [45.5%], filgrastim: 169 [54.5%]). After IPTW adjustment, standardized mean differences for all covariates were <0.1, confirming balance between the groups.

Efficacy & Safety Outcomes

Febrile Neutropenia (FN) Incidence:

  • Unadjusted: Pegfilgrastim 6.3% (9/141) vs. Filgrastim 18.3% (31/169).
  • IPTW Adjusted: Pegfilgrastim 6.3% vs. Filgrastim 17.3% (Odds Ratio [OR]: 0.32, 95% CI: 0.14-0.74).

Secondary Outcomes:

  • In-hospital Mortality:
    • Pegfilgrastim: 0% (0/141).
    • Filgrastim: 1.8% (8/169)*.
  • Median Length of Hospital Stay (IPTW Adjusted):
    • Pegfilgrastim: 9 days (IQR: 4.5-13.5).
    • Filgrastim: 17 days (IQR: 13.5-20.5) (significantly shorter for pegfilgrastim).

G-CSF Administration:

  • Median Day of Initiation: Pegfilgrastim Day 2 (IQR: 2,2) vs. Filgrastim Day 6 (IQR: 4,7) (earlier for pegfilgrastim).
  • Administration Duration: Pegfilgrastim single dose vs. Filgrastim median 7 days (IQR: 6,9).

*Abstract states "eight deaths (8/169, 1.8%)"; 8/169 is ~4.7%, while 1.8% of 169 is ~3. Displaying reported percentage.

Summary & Conclusion

  • Pegfilgrastim had a lower FN incidence and a shorter hospital stay compared to filgrastim in newly diagnosed DLBCL patients receiving the first cycle of Pola-R-CHP.
