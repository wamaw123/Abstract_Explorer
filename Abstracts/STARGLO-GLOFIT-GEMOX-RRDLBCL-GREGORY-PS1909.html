Population & Aim (N=274)

ASCT-ineligible R/R DLBCL Pts (≥1 prior LOT). Aim: Updated efficacy (OS, PFS, CR) & safety of Glofit-GemOx vs R-GemOx (STARGLO Ph III).

Glofit-GemOx (n=183; 8 cycles + 4 Glofit mono) vs R-GemOx (n=91; 8 cycles). Obi pre-tx for Glofit. Glofit step-up C1, then 30mg Q21D.

PS1909 - Glofitamab plus gemcitabine and oxaliplatin (Glofit-GemOx) in patients with relapsed/refractory (R/R) diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL): 2-year follow-up of STARGLO

Gareth Gregory, Matthew Ku, Mark Hertzberg, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PS1909 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Glofitamab (CD20xCD3 bispecific Ab) showed durable responses as fixed-duration monotherapy in R/R DLBCL (≥2 prior LOT).
  • Phase III STARGLO trial: Glofitamab + GemOx (Glofit-GemOx) demonstrated OS and PFS benefit over Rituximab + GemOx (R-GemOx) in ASCT-ineligible R/R DLBCL.

Aims

To present updated efficacy (OS, PFS, CR rate) and safety of Glofit-GemOx vs R-GemOx in R/R DLBCL patients (pts) after ≥1 LOT from the Phase III STARGLO trial (NCT04408638), including landmark analyses of pts in CR.

Methods & Study Design

  • Trial: STARGLO (Phase III, randomized, NCT04408638).
  • Patients (N=274): ASCT-ineligible R/R DLBCL, ≥1 prior LOT. Randomized 2:1.
    • Glofit-GemOx arm (n=183): 8 cycles GemOx + Glofitamab, then 4 cycles Glofitamab monotherapy.
    • R-GemOx arm (n=91): 8 cycles R-GemOx.
  • Stratification: Number of prior LOT (1 vs ≥2), refractoriness to last therapy.
  • Glofitamab Dosing: Obinutuzumab pretreatment. C1: weekly step-up (2.5mg, 10mg). From C2 D1: 30mg target dose Q21D.
  • Eligibility Note: Pts with only 1 prior LOT must have been ASCT-ineligible.
  • Primary Endpoint: OS. Secondary: IRC-assessed PFS, CR rate. Landmark analysis of pts in CR at End of Treatment (EOT).
  • Data Cutoff: June 17, 2024 (Median follow-up: 24.7 months).

STARGLO Phase III Trial Design

Patient Characteristics (N=274)

  • Baseline characteristics unchanged from primary analysis and well-balanced.
  • 1 Prior LOT: 62.8% (172 pts).
  • ≥2 Prior LOT: 37.2% (102 pts).
  • Primary Refractory: 55.8% (153 pts).
  • Refractory to Last Therapy: 60.6% (166 pts).

Efficacy Results (2-Year Follow-Up)

Glofit-GemOx vs R-GemOx:

  • Median OS: Not Evaluable (NE) vs 13.5 months (HR 0.60, 95% CI: 0.42–0.85).
  • Median IRC-PFS: 13.8 months vs 3.6 months (HR 0.41, 95% CI: 0.29–0.58).
  • CR Rate: 58.5% vs 25.3%.

Glofit-GemOx Treated Patients in CR (n=107):

  • Median Duration of CR (mDoCR): Not Reached (95% CI: 27.2–NE).
  • Median CR Follow-up: 18.2 months (range: 15.2–19.3).

Patients in CR at EOT with Glofit-GemOx (n=82):

  • OS Rate at 1 year post-EOT: 89.3%.
  • PFS Rate at 1 year post-EOT: 82.4%.

Safety Summary (Glofit-GemOx Arm)

  • Safety profile unchanged from primary analysis.
  • Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS) - Most Common AE:
    • Grade 1: 32.0%
    • Grade 2: 10.5%
    • Grade 3: 2.3%
  • Events consistent with ICANS: 4 patients (all concurrent with CRS; most Gr 1–2 [n=3]).
  • Exploratory biomarker and immune recovery data will be presented.

Summary & Conclusion

  • With 2 years of follow-up, Glofit-GemOx continued to demonstrate a clinically meaningful and superior benefit in OS and PFS compared to R-GemOx in ASCT-ineligible patients with R/R DLBCL.
  • High CR rates were achieved with Glofit-GemOx, and most patients (82%) in CR at EOT remained in remission at 1 year post-EOT.
  • The safety profile of Glofit-GemOx was consistent with the known risks of each drug, with CRS being the most common AE.
  • Updated analyses confirm durable remissions and maintained OS benefit with fixed-duration Glofit-GemOx in this patient population.
