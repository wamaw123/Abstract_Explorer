CHEMO-FREE REGIMENS CONTAINING GLOFITAMAB ARE SIGNIFICANTLY BENEFICIAL FOR R/R DLBCL PATIENTS WITH TP53 GENE MUTATIONS

Real-World Data from Multi-Centers in China

Xia Zhao, et al. | EHA Library PB3271 | Abstract Release: 05/14/25 | Presentation: 06/12/2025

Background

  • TP53 gene mutations are an independent risk factor for poor OS in DLBCL, even with 1L R-CHOP or Pola-R-CHP.
  • Relapse/refractory episodes are common in these patients.
  • No standard regimen exists for R/R DLBCL patients with TP53 gene mutations.

Study Aims

To investigate the clinical efficacy and adverse events of chemo-free regimens containing glofitamab for patients with R/R DLBCL with TP53 gene mutations.

Methods & Study Design

  • Retrospective, multi-center study (3 hospitals in China).
  • Enrollment period: April 2024 - February 2025.
  • Pts: R/R DLBCL with TP53 gene mutations (exons 5-8 detected by NGS).
  • Response assessment: PET-CT by local investigators.
