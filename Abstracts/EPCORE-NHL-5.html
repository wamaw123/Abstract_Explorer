DURABLE EFFICACY WITH FIXED-DURATION EPCORITAMAB + POLA-R-CHP FOR 1L DLBCL (EPCORE NHL-5)

Phase 1b/2 Dose Escalation/Expansion Study

Daniel Kerr, et al. | EHA Library S247 | Abstract Release: 05/14/25 | Presentation: 06/15/2025

Background

  • Standard of care (SOC) for 1L DLBCL (R-CHOP) is evolving; Pola-R-CHP showed higher CRR (78%) & longer PFS.
  • Need for therapies to further improve cure rates in 1L DLBCL.
  • Epcoritamab (subcutaneous CD3×CD20 bispecific Ab) approved for 3L+ R/R DLBCL.
  • Epcor safe & efficacious with R-CHOP (CRR 87%) and Pola-R-CHP (CRR 89%) in previous studies.
  • Current report: 16.1 mos f/u from Epcor + Pola-R-CHP cohort of EPCORE NHL-5 (NCT05283720).

Study Aims

To report updated safety, tolerability, and antitumor activity data of epcoritamab + Pola-R-CHP in patients with newly diagnosed DLBCL.

Methods & Study Design

  • Phase 1b/2 dose escalation/expansion study (EPCORE NHL-5).
  • Pts: Newly diagnosed CD20+ DLBCL (NOS, HGBCL DH/TH, FL G3B), ECOG PS 0-2, IPI 2-5.
  • Key Endpoints: Investigator-assessed ORR/CRR, TTR/TTCR, safety.
  • Exploratory: Biomarkers, PK.

Summary & Conclusion

  • Fixed-duration epcoritamab + Pola-R-CHP (16.1 mos f/u) in 1L DLBCL continues to show high ORR and CRR across all subgroups.
  • No new safety signals were observed.
  • Data support that adding epcoritamab to 1L SOC drives deep and durable responses.
  • CRS was low grade with predictable timing of onset. No ICANS observed.
  • These data reaffirm prior findings, demonstrate versatility of subcutaneous epcoritamab, and support the potential of this combination for 1L DLBCL.
