PF628 - Myeloid neoplasms following CAR T-cell therapy: The role of prior therapies and clonal evolution

Danielle Hammond, Koji Sasaki, Koichi Takahashi, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PF628 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Secondary myeloid neoplasms (MN) are a reported risk following chimeric antigen receptor (CAR) T-cell therapy.
  • Understanding the contribution of prior therapies and clonal evolution to MN development is crucial.

Methods & Study Design

  • Study Type: Retrospective review of consecutive MN cases.
  • Institution: Single center.
  • Enrollment: Patients diagnosed with MN after receiving commercially available CAR T-cell therapy for non-myeloid hematologic malignancies since 2015.
  • CAR-T Products: CD19- or BCMA-targeting, with fludarabine-cyclophosphamide lymphodepletion.
  • Prior Therapy Assessment: Autologous stem cell transplant (autoSCT) treated as a unique line of therapy (LoT).
  • Clonal Evolution Analysis: Comparison of bone marrow samples within 12 months prior to CAR T and at MN diagnosis.

Patient & CAR-T Characteristics (Pre-MN)

  • Estimated MN Incidence: 2.5%.
  • Primary Malignancy: Non-Hodgkin B-cell lymphoma (79%), Multiple Myeloma (MM, 21%).
  • Median Age at CAR-T: 65 years (IQR 61-69).
  • Median Prior Lines of Therapy (LoT): 5 (range 1-11).
  • Prior autoSCT: 44% (BEAM or melphalan conditioning).
  • Prior Alkylating Agent Exposure: 100%.
  • Prior Lenalidomide Exposure: 41%.

Results: Myeloid Neoplasm (MN) Characteristics (N=39)

MN Details:

  • Types: 29 MDS, 1 CMML, 9 AML.
  • Male: 72%.
  • Median Baseline BM Blasts: 6% (IQR 1-14).
  • Marrow Ring Sideroblasts (≥2%): 46%.
  • Adverse Cytogenetics: Common (Complex karyotype 44%, del(7/7q) 62%, MECOM-rearranged 13%).
  • Multi-hit TP53 Perturbations (ICC 2022): 46%.

Latency to MN Diagnosis:

  • Post CAR-T: Median 16 months (IQR 9-27); 39% within 12 months.
  • Post Alkylating Agents: Median 4.9 years.
  • Post autoSCT: Median 5.3 years.
  • Post Lenalidomide: Median 5.3 years.

MN Treatment & Outcomes:

  • Supportive Care Only: 30%.
  • Hypomethylating Agents: 64% of treated patients.
  • Response to MN Therapy (n=27): CR 7% (2 pts), CRi 15% (4 pts).
  • AlloSCT for MN (n=6): 3 died (infection/GVHD); 3 survived with durable remission (2 TP53 WT).
  • Median OS from MN Diagnosis: 11.0 months (95% CI 6.1, 16.2).
  • Prior Lenalidomide Exposure: Improved mOS post-MN (14.3 vs 7.4 mo, p=0.038).
  • AlloSCT: Trend for improved mOS (14.3 vs 9.0 mo, p=0.220).
  • No significant mOS association with sex, age ≥65, prior autoSCT, ≥3 pre-CAR T LoT, or TP53 status.

Clonal Evolution:

  • Of 7 pts with TP53 sequencing pre-CAR T, 5 (71%) had TP53 mutations, all showing clonal expansion and/or second allelic hits at MN diagnosis.

Summary & Conclusion

  • The estimated incidence of myeloid neoplasms (MN) post-CAR T-cell therapy was 2.5% in this cohort.
  • The cytomolecular drivers (e.g., adverse cytogenetics, TP53 perturbations) and the ~5-year latency from pre-CAR T therapies (alkylating agents, autoSCT, lenalidomide) to MN diagnosis align with historical characteristics of therapy-related MN.
  • These findings underscore the significant role of prior therapy exposures in the malignant transformation leading to MN post-CAR T.
  • Clonal evolution of pre-existing mutations (e.g., TP53) was observed.
  • Survival after MN diagnosis is poor, though prior lenalidomide exposure was associated with improved mOS.
