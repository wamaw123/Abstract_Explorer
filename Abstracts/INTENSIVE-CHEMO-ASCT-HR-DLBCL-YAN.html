Visual Summary: PB3322 - Intensive Chemo & ASCT in High-Intermediate/High-Risk DLBCL

Population & Aim (N=19)

High-intermediate/High-risk DLBCL (IPI ≥2). Aim: Eval efficacy/safety of intensive chemo + ASCT.

Intervention (2 Groups)

Transplant (N=12): R-HyperCVAD x2 → R-DICE x3 → ASCT. Chemo (N=7): R-HyperCVAD x2 → R-DICE x3 → R-DICE x1.

Key Efficacy (CR after C4)

Transplant GRP: 100%
Chemo GRP: 57.1%
2-yr OS/PFS (Transplant): 100%/100%

Safety & Conclusion

Hematologic toxicity prominent (G3/4 Neutropenia, Thrombocytopenia). ASCT may benefit survival.

PB3322 - Efficacy and safety of sequential intensive chemotherapy and autologous hematopoietic stem cell transplantation in the treatment of high-intermediate- or high-risk diffuse large B-cell lymphoma

BeiBei Gao, XinNan Chen, YaNan Huang, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PB3322 | Publication Only

Background

  • High-risk DLBCL has high malignancy, strong proliferation, often with complex molecular features (double/triple-hit).
  • ASCT after intensive chemotherapy can prolong survival and reduce recurrence in high-risk patients.
  • Lack of large-scale, high-quality clinical data on long-term efficacy/safety of this approach.

Aims

To evaluate the efficacy and safety of sequential intensive chemotherapy and ASCT in high-intermediate- or high-risk DLBCL patients.

Methods & Study Design

Prospective, open, multicenter, single-arm clinical study.

  • Primary Endpoint: 2-year PFS.
  • Secondary Endpoints: 2-year OS, CR rate after induction.
  • Patients (N=19) divided into Transplant group (N=12) and Chemotherapy group (N=7).

Efficacy Results (Median F/U: 17.4 months)

Response after 2nd Cycle Chemo:

  • Transplant Group (N=12): CR 8.3% (1 Pt), PR 83.3% (10 Pts), SD 8.3% (1 Pt)
  • Chemotherapy Group (N=7): PR 100% (7 Pts)

Response after 4th Cycle Chemo (pre-transplant for ASCT group):

  • Transplant Group (N=12): CR 100% (all Pts achieved CR before ASCT; 2 Pts achieved CR after 3 cycles R-DICE)
  • Chemotherapy Group (N=7): CR 57.1% (4 Pts), PR 28.6% (2 Pts), PD 14.3% (1 Pt)

Survival Outcomes:

  • Median OS: Not reached in either group.
  • 2-year OS: Transplant Group 100% vs. Chemotherapy Group 68.6%
  • 2-year PFS: Transplant Group 100% vs. Chemotherapy Group 80%

Safety Summary (N=19, 90 total chemo cycles)

  • Hematological toxicity was prominent.
  • Grade 3-4 Neutropenia: Occurred in 47/90 cycles.
  • Grade 3-4 Thrombocytopenia: Occurred in 27/90 cycles.
  • Grade 3 Infection: Occurred in 11/90 cycles.
  • No infection-related deaths during chemotherapy or transplantation.

Summary & Conclusion

  • CR rate after 4th cycle was significantly higher in the transplantation group vs. chemotherapy group (100% vs. 57.1%).
  • 2-year OS and PFS were 100% vs. 68.6% and 100% vs. 80% for transplant vs. chemotherapy groups, respectively.
  • Results suggest that transplantation treatment may benefit the survival of patients with high-intermediate/high-risk DLBCL.
