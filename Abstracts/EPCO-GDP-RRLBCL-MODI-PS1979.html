Population & Aim (N=14 enrolled)

RR LBCL Pts (≥1 prior LOT), eligible for CAR T/SCT. Aim: Evaluate safety & efficacy of Epco-GDP as bridge/salvage.

Intervention (Epco-GDP, 3 Cycles)

Epco SC (step-up C1, then weekly) + GDP (Gem, Cis, Dexa). Outpatient. Then CAR T/SCT or Epco mono.

CRS 50% (all G1-2), no ICANS. Manageable AEs (Thrombocytopenia G3/4 85.7%). Early efficacy signal, feasible outpatient bridge.

PS1979 - Epcoritamab with gemcitabine, dexamethasone, and cisplatin (Epco-GDP) in relapsed refractory large B-cell lymphoma- An interim analysis of Phase II multicenter investigated-initiated trial

Dipenkumar Modi, Vy Ong, Seongho Kim, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PS1979 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Patients (pts) with primary refractory or relapsed large B-cell lymphoma (LBCL) receive CAR T-cell or autologous stem cell transplant (SCT) as curative therapy.
  • Rapidly progressing, symptomatic pts often need bridging treatment while awaiting CAR T-cell therapy.
  • Salvage/Bridging chemotherapy with high response rates is warranted.
  • Epcoritamab (CD3xCD20 bispecific antibody) is approved for relapsed/refractory (RR) LBCL after ≥2 prior lines of therapy.
  • Hypothesis: Epcoritamab + Gemcitabine, Dexamethasone, Cisplatin (Epco-GDP) improves outcomes in RR LBCL eligible for CAR T/SCT.

Aims

To evaluate the safety and efficacy of the Epco-GDP regimen in RR LBCL patients.

Methods & Study Design

  • Trial Type: Phase II multicenter investigator-initiated trial (interim analysis).
  • Patient Population: CD20+ RR LBCL, ≥1 prior line of therapy (including anti-CD20 Ab), eligible for CAR T-cell or SCT.
  • Treatment Regimen (Epco-GDP, 3 cycles, outpatient, 21-day cycles):
    • Epcoritamab (Epco) SC: C1 weekly step-up (0.16mg, 0.8mg, 48mg); C2 onwards weekly 48mg.
    • GDP: Gemcitabine 1000 mg/m² (Days 1, 8); Cisplatin 75 mg/m² (Day 1); Dexamethasone 40mg PO (Days 1-4).
  • Post C3: Pts could proceed to CAR T/SCT per investigator discretion. Those not proceeding could receive Epco monotherapy (6 additional 28-day cycles, C4-C9) or until toxicity/PD.
  • Primary Objective: Complete Remission (CR) rate per Lugano criteria after 3 cycles.
  • Data Cutoff: November 30, 2024.

Patient Characteristics (N=14)

  • Median Age: 63 years (range, 43-72).
  • Stage IV Disease: 86%.
  • DLBCL, NOS Subtype: 86%.
  • Double Expressor Lymphoma: 21%.
  • Double Hit Lymphoma: 7%.
  • Primary Refractory Disease: 43%.
  • Median Prior Lines of Therapy: 2 (range, 1-4).

Efficacy Results (Interim Analysis, N=14)

All patients received 3 cycles of Epco-GDP.

  • Overall Response Rate (ORR): 78.6%
  • Complete Remission (CR) Rate: 64.3%
  • Successful stem cell mobilization or T-lymphocyte collection in all patients.
  • Post-study treatment: 8 underwent autologous SCT, 5 underwent CAR T-cell therapy.
  • Median follow-up for survival: 7.7 months (95% CI, 6 to 10.6).
  • At 6-months:
    • Duration of Response (DOR): 100%.
    • Progression-Free Survival (PFS): 73.9%.
    • Overall Survival (OS): 100%.

Safety Summary (N=14)

  • Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS): 7 (50%) pts, all Grade 1-2.
  • No ICANS observed.
  • Hospitalization for CRS or fever: 17%.
  • Adverse Events (AEs) in ≥20% of pts (any grade):
    • Thrombocytopenia: 92.9%
    • Anemia: 71.4%
    • Neutropenia: 50%
    • Nausea: 42.9%
    • Acute Kidney Injury (AKI): 28.6%
    • Febrile Neutropenia: 21.4%
    • Diarrhea: 21.4%
    • Tinnitus: 28.6%
    • AST elevation: 28.6%
    • ALT elevation: 50%
    • ALP elevation: 28.6%
  • Grade 3-4 AEs in ≥20% of pts:
    • Thrombocytopenia: 85.7%
    • Anemia: 50%
    • Neutropenia: 35.7%
    • AKI: 21.4%
    • Febrile Neutropenia: 21.4%

Summary & Conclusion

  • The Epco-GDP regimen shows an early signal of efficacy in RR LBCL, with high ORR and CR rates after 3 cycles.
  • This regimen is feasible to administer in the outpatient setting as a bridge or salvage therapy prior to CAR T-cell therapy or SCT.
  • Epco-GDP did not adversely impact stem cell mobilization or T-lymphocyte collection.
  • The safety profile, including CRS and hematologic AEs, appears manageable.
  • The study is continuing enrollment towards a final accrual goal of 32 patients.
