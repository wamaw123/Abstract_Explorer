PF1319 - LYMPHOVISTA - A validated platform for genotyping and MRD assessment in different types of lymphoma

Julia Mattlener, Jessica Schneider, Julia K. Schleifenbaum, Sophie Heidenreich, Hodgkin Lymphoma MRD Consortium, Helen Kaul, Gundolf Schneider, Peter Borchmann, Jan-Michel Heger, Sven Borchmann

EHA Library PF1319 | Abstract Release: 05/14/25 | Presentation: 06/13/2025

Background

  • Circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) sequencing has advanced genotyping and minimal residual disease (MRD) assessment in lymphoma.
  • Current assays often lack validation and comparability.
  • The LymphoVista-Platform was designed and validated for ctDNA sequencing in B-cell lymphoma, compatible with small sequencing instruments for cost-effective, rapid clinical use.
  • LymphoVista covers diverse B-cell lymphomas, while LymphoVista HL is specialized for Hodgkin Lymphoma (HL).

Aims

To develop an enhanced and validated ctDNA sequencing platform to facilitate de-novo variant calling and MRD assessment in lymphoma.

Methods & Study Design

  • LymphoVista Platform: Targets 79 genes (~167 kbp), includes modules for MRD assessment, de-novo variant calling, and validation targets.
  • LymphoVista HL Platform: Targets 37 genes (~83 kbp), specialized for HL.
  • Technical Validation: Performed with contrived samples (reference standard diluted in healthy donor cfDNA) to evaluate analytical performance.
  • Clinical Samples (LymphoVista): Follicular lymphoma (FL, n=19), marginal zone lymphoma (MZL, n=6), large B-cell Lymphoma (LBCL, n=3), central nervous system lymphoma (CNSL, n=3).
  • Clinical Validation (LymphoVista HL): Blinded study in 72 HL patients from the HD21 trial, assessing MRD after two treatment cycles (MRD-2) for prognostic value for PFS. MRD-2 positivity defined as levels above LoD.

Results

LymphoVista Performance:

  • De-novo variant detection (≥0.5% mAF): Sensitivity 93.59%, Specificity 99.99%.
  • MRD assessment LoD: 6.69 x 10-6, with 100% specificity, sensitivity, and accuracy for MRD levels > 3.04 x 10-5.
  • Clinical samples: Exonic/splicing mutations in core cancer genes (e.g., BCL2, EZH2, PIM1, TP53, IGLL5) identified in 100% of MZL, LBCL, CNSL, and 95% of FL patients.

LymphoVista HL Performance:

  • De-novo variant detection (≥0.5% mAF): Sensitivity 91.27%, Specificity 99.99%.
  • MRD assessment LoD: 6.54 x 10-6, with 100% specificity, sensitivity, and accuracy for MRD levels > 1.76 x 10-5.
  • Robust reproducibility at low MRD levels across varying operating conditions (operator, time, kit batch) for both panels.
  • Clinical Validation (HD21 Trial, N=72 HL patients):
    • MRD-2 positive: 18.5% of patients.
    • 4-year PFS significantly longer in MRD-2 negative vs. MRD-2 positive patients: 95.3% (95% CI: 88.7-100) vs. 72.2% (95% CI: 41.5-100).
    • Hazard Ratio (HR) for PFS: 6.9 (95% CI: 4.5-10.6), p < 0.0001.

Progression Free Survival by MRD-2 status in HL patients (HD21 trial).

Summary & Conclusion

  • The LymphoVista platform, featuring two validated ctDNA sequencing assays, enables ultrasensitive genotyping and MRD assessment in B-cell lymphoma.
  • Validation confirms high accuracy, specificity, sensitivity, and prognostic value of the platform.
  • LymphoVista HL reliably predicts outcomes in HL, paving the way for MRD-guided clinical trials and clinical application.

Keywords

B cell lymphoma, Minimal residual disease (MRD), ctDNA, liquid biopsy.
