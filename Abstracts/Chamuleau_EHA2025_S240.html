PhasED-Seq Methodology

    [cite_start]
  • PhasED-Seq is an ultrasensitive sequencing method for detecting ctDNA-based minimal residual disease (MRD)[cite: 3].
    • [cite_start]
  • It first identifies patient-specific phased variants (multiple mutations on the same DNA fragment) from tumor tissue[cite: 4].
    • [cite_start]
  • It then tracks these specific variants in blood (cell-free DNA) at the end of treatment to detect residual disease with high accuracy[cite: 4].
    • [cite_start]
  • The analytical limit of detection (LOD95) is 0.7 parts per million[cite: 4].

MRD Adds Value to PET-CT

[cite_start]

In patients with a complete metabolic response (CMR) by PET scan, ctDNA-MRD status identified a subgroup with poor outcomes[cite: 13].

    [cite_start]
  • MRD-negative + CMR: 3-year PFS 89% [cite: 13]
    • [cite_start]
  • MRD-positive + CMR: 3-year PFS 36% (HR=6.58) [cite: 13]
[cite_start]

In patients without a CMR, ctDNA-MRD identified an even higher-risk group[cite: 12].

    [cite_start]
  • MRD-negative + no CMR: 3-year PFS 64% [cite: 12]
    • [cite_start]
  • MRD-positive + no CMR: 3-year PFS 4% (HR=6.78) [cite: 12]

Independent Prognostic Value

[cite_start]

In a multivariate analysis for Progression-Free Survival (PFS), ctDNA-MRD was a more powerful and independent predictor of outcome than both IPI score and EOT PET-CT results[cite: 11].

    [cite_start]
  • EOT ctDNA MRD+: HR 9.66 (p=3.17e-08) [cite: 11]
    • [cite_start]
  • EOT PET-CT+: HR 4.0 (p=0.000374) [cite: 11]
    • [cite_start]
  • IPI High Risk: Not significant (p=0.316) [cite: 11]

Conclusions

    [cite_start]
  • End-of-treatment ctDNA-MRD assessment by PhasED-Seq is highly prognostic for both PFS and OS in 1L LBCL patients from a real-world setting[cite: 15].
    • [cite_start]
  • ctDNA-MRD provides independent evidence of residual disease beyond PET-CT and is a more powerful prognostic tool than both PET-CT and IPI score[cite: 15].
    • [cite_start]
  • MRD status can further stratify risk in patients both with and without a complete metabolic response by PET, identifying patients with especially poor outcomes[cite: 12, 13].
    • [cite_start]
  • The data support the integration of ctDNA-MRD as a standard component of response evaluation in 1L LBCL[cite: 15].
