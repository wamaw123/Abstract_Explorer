Population & Aim

DLBCL patients (N=107). Aim: Investigate the role of CD47 expression in systemic inflammation (NLR).

Methodology

Retrospective cohort. CD47 by IHC (low/high). NLR from records (low/high by ROC cut-off 3.43). Response by RECIL.

Key Findings

CD47high assoc. w/ high NLR (70.2% vs 50.8%, p=0.044). CD47high & high NLR assoc. w/ lower CR rates (CD47: 33.3% vs 62.1%, p=0.003; NLR: 41.3% vs 61.9%, p=0.038).

CD47 expression is upregulated in DLBCL, associated with treatment response. Stronger CD47 expression contributes to augmented inflammation. Targeting CD47 promising.

PB3312: Association of CD47 expression and systemic inflammation in diffuse large B-cell lymphoma patients

Nia Siregar, Cosphiadi Irawan, Maria Ham, Ikhwan Rinaldi

EHA Library PB3312 | Abstract Release: 05/14/2025 | Publication Only: 06/12/2025

Background

  • CD47, a macrophage immune checkpoint, is part of the complex tumor microenvironment (TME) in lymphoma.
  • The TME is modified by inflammatory cells; systemic inflammatory response and host immunity are important in tumor progression.

Aims

This study aimed to reveal the role of CD47 expression in systemic inflammation of diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) patients.

Methods & Study Design

  • Study Type: Retrospective cohort study.
  • Patient Population: DLBCL patients from 4 hospitals (Jan 2017 - Aug 2021).
  • Systemic Inflammation Marker: Neutrophil-Lymphocyte Ratio (NLR) obtained from medical records. NLR grouped by ROC curve cut-off.
  • CD47 Expression: Immunohistochemical (IHC) staining on formalin-fixed paraffin-embedded (FFPE) tumor tissues.
    • Assessed on membrane and/or cytoplasm of tumor cells.
    • Grouped: CD47low (score 0-2) and CD47high (score 3-4).
  • Treatment Response: Assessed using RECIL criteria.

Key Findings (N=107)

CD47 Expression & NLR:

  • CD47 group was significantly associated with NLR group (p=0.044).
  • Higher proportion of subjects with high NLR (>3.43) in CD47high group vs. CD47low group (70.2% vs. 50.8%).

Summary & Conclusion

  • CD47 expression is upregulated in DLBCL patients and is associated with treatment response.
  • Stronger expression of CD47 (CD47high) contributes to augmented systemic inflammatory responses (higher NLR) in DLBCL.
  • Both CD47high status and high NLR are associated with a lower proportion of patients achieving complete remission.
  • These findings suggest that targeting CD47 might be a promising treatment strategy for DLBCL.
