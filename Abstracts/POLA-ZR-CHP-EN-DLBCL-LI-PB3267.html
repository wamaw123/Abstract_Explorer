PB3267 - Outcomes of zanubrutinib combined with polatuzumab vedotin, rituximab, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, and prednisone (POLA-ZR-CHP) in the treatment of patients with extranodal (EN) diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) in a real-world setting

Caixia Li, Yuchen Hua, Sixun Jia, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PB3267 | Publication Only

Background

  • Patients with multiple extranodal involvement diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL) have a poor response rate to standard R-CHOP treatment.
  • This study assesses the outcomes of DLBCL patients treated with Zanubrutinib in combination with Polatuzumab Vedotin, Rituximab, Cyclophosphamide, Doxorubicin, and Prednisone (Pola-ZR-CHP) as either first- or second-line therapy.

Aims

To assess the outcomes of DLBCL patients treated with Zanubrutinib in combination with Pola-R-CHP as either first- or second-line therapy in a real-world setting.

Methods & Study Design

  • Study Type: Single-center, retrospective study.
  • Institution: First Affiliated Hospital of Soochow University.
  • Patient Population: Adult patients (aged ≥18 years) diagnosed with DLBCL, who received Pola-ZR-CHP between August 2023 and January 2025 as first- or second-line therapy.
  • Treatment Regimen: Polatuzumab Vedotin, Zanubrutinib, Rituximab, Cyclophosphamide, Doxorubicin, and Prednisone (Pola-ZR-CHP). (Specific dosing for Zanubrutinib or Pola-R-CHP components not detailed in abstract).
  • Endpoints: Overall response rate (ORR), complete response (CR) rate (Lugano criteria), and progression-free survival (PFS).

Efficacy Results (Median F/U: 6 months)

All patients completed ≥1 cycle of Pola-ZR-CHP; median 5 cycles (range 1-6). Total 47 cycles administered.

Response After 3 Cycles (N=9 evaluable)

  • ORR: 100% (9/9)
  • CR Rate: 33% (3/9)

Response at End of Treatment (EOT, N=6 evaluable)

  • All TP53 unmutated patients achieved remission.
  • One of two TP53 mutated patients experienced disease progression.
  • Disease progression occurred in 2 patients overall. Two patients died.
  • Estimated Median PFS: 27.5 months.
  • Estimated 12-month PFS Rate: 66.7%.

Safety Summary (N=11)

The Pola-ZR-CHP regimen was generally well-tolerated.

Most Common Adverse Events (AEs):

  • Hematologic toxicities were most common.
  • Neutropenia: 7 patients.
  • Thrombocytopenia: 5 patients.
  • Pneumonia: 4 patients.
  • No treatment-related deaths were observed.

Summary & Conclusion

  • The combination of Zanubrutinib and Pola-R-CHP demonstrates promising efficacy and good safety in both previously untreated and R/R extranodal DLBCL patients.
  • These findings suggest that Pola-ZR-CHP may offer significant survival benefits for this patient population.
  • Further studies with larger cohorts and extended follow-up are warranted to validate these results and explore the long-term outcomes of this novel therapeutic regimen.
