Visual Summary: PB3488 - Comparing ICAHT Predictive Models (CAR-HT, ALL-HT, eIPM) Post CAR-T

Population & Aim (N=85)

Pts with hematological malignancies (B-ALL, T-ALL/LBL, MM, AML) post CAR-T. Aim: Compare predictive performance of CAR-HT, ALL-HT, eIPM for ICAHT.

Methods

Prospective analysis (2022-24). Calculated CAR-HT, ALL-HT, eIPM scores in acute leukemia & MM pts receiving CAR-T.

Key Findings (ICAHT Prediction)

All 3 scores identified high-risk ICAHT pts with prolonged neutropenia (e.g., CAR-HT: 18.4 vs 4.2 days, p < 1e-6). eIPM scores lower in ICAHT G<3 (p < 1e-6).

Conclusion (Survival & Utility)

Scores predict ICAHT well. For survival, integration with other parameters needed. ALL-HT high-risk B-ALL: shorter 1-yr DFS (36% vs 76%, p=0.031).

PB3488 - Can one scoring system fit all? A comparative evaluation of CAR-HEMATOTOX, ALL-HT, and EIPM for predicting immune effector cell-associated hematotoxicity (ICAHT) after CAR-T in hematological malignancies

Ao-ran Zhang, Shang Qiannan, Xian-Ying Yin, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PB3488 | Publication Only

Background

  • Immune effector cell-associated hematotoxicity (ICAHT) significantly impacts survival after CAR-T therapy.
  • Existing predictive models:
    • CAR-HEMATOTOX (CAR-HT): For lymphoma and myeloma.
    • ALL-HEMATOTOX (ALL-HT): For B-cell Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (B-ALL).
    • Early ICAHT Prediction Model (eIPM).
  • These models have not undergone systematic comparative evaluation across various hematological malignancies.

Aims

This prospective analysis aimed to compare the predictive performance of three ICAHT models (CAR-HT, ALL-HT, eIPM) across different hematological malignancies.

Methods & Study Design

  • Study Type: Prospective analysis.
  • Enrollment: Consecutive patients with hematological malignancies receiving commercial or experimental CAR-T cell therapy (2022-2024).
  • Patient Cohort (N=85):
    • B-ALL (N=35): CD19 CAR-T (14 KTE-19/Axicel).
    • T-ALL/LBL (N=17): CD7 CAR-T.
    • Multiple Myeloma (MM) (N=28): BCMA CAR-T.
    • Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML) (N=5): LILRB4 cell therapy.
  • Scoring: CAR-HT, ALL-HT, and eIPM scores calculated for patients with acute leukemia and MM.
  • Endpoints: Duration of neutropenia, incidence of ICAHT (grade >2), Overall Survival (OS), Disease-Free Survival (DFS).

Results: Predictive Performance of ICAHT Models

Total enrolled patients: 85. Median cumulative duration of ANC <500/μL (first 30 days): 14 days. 46 patients (54.1%) experienced ICAHT > Grade 2. No significant association found between ICAHT scores and severe CRS (≥G2) or ICANS (≥G2).

Neutropenia Duration by Risk Group:

  • CAR-HT (N=85): High-risk (n=66, 77.6%) vs. Low-risk (n=19, 22.4%)
    • Duration: 18.4 vs. 4.2 days (p < 1×10⁻⁶).
  • ALL-HT (N=52 ALL/LBL): High-risk (n=34, 65.4%) vs. Low-risk (n=18, 34.6%)
    • Duration: 22.1 vs. 6.9 days (p < 1×10⁻⁶).
    • B-ALL Subgroup: 23.5 vs. 8.1 days (p < 0.0001).
    • T-ALL/LBL Subgroup: 18.7 vs. 5.1 days (p < 0.01).

eIPM Scoring System (N=85):

  • Patients with ICAHT grade <3 had significantly lower scores than those with ICAHT grade 3-4 in:
    • eIPMpre model (p < 1×10⁻⁷).
    • eIPMpost model (p < 1×10⁻⁶).
  • (Abstract notes specific percentages for eIPMpre [36.9% vs 17.0%] and eIPMpost [36.3% vs 15.7%], likely referring to proportions of patients within score categories achieving certain ICAHT outcomes, confirming lower scores for less severe ICAHT).

Survival Outcomes (1-year):

  • CAR-HT Cohort:
    • OS: High-risk 79% vs. Low-risk 73% (p=0.6, NS).
    • DFS: High-risk 49% vs. Low-risk 71% (p=0.068, trend).
  • ALL-HT Cohort (ALL/LBL Pts):
    • OS: High-risk 69% vs. Low-risk 63% (p=0.5, NS).
    • DFS: High-risk 34% vs. Low-risk 58% (p=0.071, trend).
    • DFS in B-ALL (ALL-HT): High-risk 36% vs. Low-risk 76% (p=0.031, significant).

Summary & Conclusion

  • CAR-HT, ALL-HT, and eIPM scores effectively identified patients at high risk for ICAHT with prolonged neutropenia post-CAR-T infusion across acute leukemia (B-ALL, T-ALL/LBL, AML) and Multiple Myeloma (MM).
  • The models showed utility in emerging research areas like T-ALL/LBL and AML.
  • While predictive for hematotoxicity, the prognostic utility of these scores for survival outcomes (OS, DFS) may require integration with additional parameters to enhance predictive precision.
  • Notably, high-risk B-ALL patients identified by ALL-HT had significantly shorter 1-year DFS.
