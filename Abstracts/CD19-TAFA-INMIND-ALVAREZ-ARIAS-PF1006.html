PF1006: CD19 expression is retained in patients with relapsed/refractory follicular or marginal zone lymphoma after receiving tafasitamab, lenalidomide and rituximab in the inMIND study

Sarah Reinke, Wolfram Klapper, Oscar Bortolami, TaeHyung Kim, Diana Alvarez Arias

EHA Library PF1006 | Abstract Release: 05/14/2025 | Presentation: 06/13/2025

Background

  • Frontline FL treatment often uses anti-CD20 mAbs (e.g., Rituximab [R]) Â± chemotherapy.
  • Immunotherapies like Lenalidomide (L) + R are preferred for R/R FL, but durability needs improvement.
  • CD19 is expressed in FL and is a target for therapies (e.g., CAR-T), but CD19 antigen loss is a concern for sequential CD19 targeting.
  • Tafasitamab (Tafa) is an Fc-enhanced, CD19-targeting mAb, approved with L for R/R DLBCL.
  • inMIND Study (NCT04680052): Phase 3 trial evaluating Tafa+L+R vs Placebo (Pbo)+L+R in R/R FL or MZL.
    • Met primary endpoint: prolonged PFS for Tafa+L+R in FL pts (Sehn LH. Blood. 2024; LBA1).

Aims

To explore the impact of Tafa+L+R and Pbo+L+R on CD19 and CD20 expression and how this correlates with treatment response; and to assess the impact of treatments on B-cell counts and depth of response (minimal residual disease [MRD]).

Methods (inMIND Pharmacodynamic Sub-analysis)

  • Study Design: Pharmacodynamic analysis from the Phase 3, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled inMIND trial.
  • Patient Population: Patients with R/R FL or MZL.
  • B-cell Counts: Assessed by flow cytometry (IQVIA).
  • CD19/CD20 Expression:
    • Tumor biopsies assessed centrally by Immunohistochemistry (IHC).
    • Anti-CD19 mAb: BT51E clone.
    • Anti-CD20 mAb: L26 clone.
  • CD19 Sequencing: Performed using NovaSeq6000 (NeoGenomics) on blood or BMA samples from MRD positive pts.
  • MRD Assessment: Measured in peripheral blood (PB) and bone marrow aspirate (BMA) by IgH clonality and somatic hypermutation assay (NeoGenomics).

Key Findings: CD19 & CD20 Expression

At Screening:

  • CD19 expression was 100% in 86% of screening biopsies.
  • 2 pts were CD19-negative at screening.
  • 8 pts were CD20-negative at screening.
    • Tafa+L+R arm (n=4): 4/4 responded (3 CR, 1 PR).
    • Pbo+L+R arm (n=4): 2/4 responded (1 CR, 1 PR).
  • Expression of CD19 or CD20 (alone or together) did not correlate significantly with response.

At End of Treatment (EOT) / Follow-up (N=32 samples):

  • Tumor cells detected: 62% (8/13) in Tafa+L+R arm vs 84% (16/19) in Pbo+L+R arm.
  • All EOT samples with tumor cells were CD19 positive by IHC, except 1 pt refractory to R in Tafa+L+R arm.
    • This exceptional pt (80% CD19-neg at screening) achieved PR, suggesting elimination of CD19+ cells.

CD19 Sequencing (N=28, MRD+ Tafa Arm):

  • No CD19 somatic mutations identified.

Other Findings

  • Peripheral B-cell Counts: Decreased in both arms (median 0.0210 GI/L for Tafa+L+R and 0.0315 GI/L for Pbo+L+R at baseline; 0 GI/L for each arm on treatment).
  • MRD Assessment: IgH clonality detected in 25% of PB and 34% of BMA samples. Results were inconclusive due to a small number of evaluable pts.

Summary & Conclusion

  • In patients with R/R FL or MZL enrolled in the inMIND study, analyses of biopsies and CD19 sequencing suggest that the CD19 antigen is preserved on tumor cells following treatment with Tafasitamab+L+R.
  • Neither CD19 nor CD20 expression, alone or in combination, correlated significantly with response to Tafa+L+R.
  • These findings support the potential for sequential CD19-targeted therapies in this patient population.
