PF916 - Positron emission tomography-adapted therapy in low-risk diffuse large B-cell lymphoma: 4-year results of a randomized, phase III, non-inferiority trial

Qing Shi, Di Fu, Pengpeng Xu, et al.

EHA 2025 Congress | Abstract #PF916 | Poster Presentation

Background

  • Goal: Maintain efficacy while minimizing chemotherapy in IPI low-risk, non-bulky DLBCL.
  • Previous results of this trial: Reducing 6 R-CHOP cycles to 4 achieved comparable efficacy with fewer AEs in patients with interim PET-CT confirmed CR.
  • Long-term follow-up is needed to validate these findings.

Aims

To compare four cycles of chemotherapy to six cycles in non-bulky, low-risk (IPI = 0-1) DLBCL patients with negative interim PET-CT, through extended follow-up.

Methods & Study Design

  • Open-label, randomized, phase III, non-inferiority trial (NCT02752815).
  • Patients: Aged 14-75 yrs, newly diagnosed low-risk DLBCL (IPI 0-1), non-bulky.
  • Initial Treatment: All patients received 4 cycles of R-CHOP.
  • Randomization (1:1) for PET-CT CR patients after 4 R-CHOP cycles:
    • Arm 1 (4R-CHOP+4R): 4 cycles of Rituximab.
    • Arm 2 (6R-CHOP+2R): 2 additional cycles of R-CHOP + 2 cycles of Rituximab.
  • Primary Endpoint: Progression-Free Survival (PFS).
  • Analysis: Intention-to-treat (ITT). Data cutoff: Oct 30, 2024.

Safety Summary

A total of 12 (4%) patients died:

  • 7 due to disease progression.
  • 3 due to pulmonary infection (1 during treatment, 2 during COVID-19 pandemic).
  • (Detailed AE comparison between arms not provided in this extended follow-up abstract, but previous report indicated fewer AEs with 4 cycles).

Summary & Conclusion

  • Four cycles of R-CHOP plus four cycles of rituximab provide long-term clinical efficacy comparable to six cycles of R-CHOP plus two cycles of rituximab in low-risk, non-bulky DLBCL patients with negative interim PET-CT.
  • This interim PET-adapted strategy ensures durable remission and offers new insights into the management of low-risk DLBCL.
